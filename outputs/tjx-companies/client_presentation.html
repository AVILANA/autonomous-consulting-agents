What is this?

This document presents an outside-in assessment of your supply chain operations and competitive position, built entirely from public sources — annual reports, earnings transcripts, investor presentations, and corporate disclosures. It identifies four quantified operational gaps, benchmarks your performance against 7 global peers, and tags every claim by confidence level. The goal is not to tell you what to do — it is to show what we found, ask the questions only your team can answer, and whether these gaps are worth exploring together.

Five Surprising Takeaways

1

TJX outperforms Ross on gross margin (31.0% vs 27.8%) but loses the margin advantage entirely at the operating line (11.9% vs 12.2%). Ross spends 3.5pp less converting gross profit to operating income — suggesting TJX's SG&A, freight, and DC cost structure absorbs a larger share of gross margin despite similar revenue per store. This is directly linked to P3 (freight audit) and the Gross-to-Op Margin Spread RED KPI.

2

Inditex inventory turns 8–9x vs TJX 5.6x on a $30B+ inventory base — without repeating SKUs being the bottleneck. Inditex achieves this through RFID at item level and real-time sell-through feedback into allocation. TJX's challenge is different (no repeating SKUs) but the turns gap still represents $260M–$365M in trapped working capital if closed even partially.

3

Ross generates $240K revenue per employee vs TJX $163K — 47% more productive on the same off-price model. Ross operates US-only, which simplifies HR and labor management. But Ross is also investing $1.1B in FY2026 capital expenditure explicitly in AI supply chain and store technology. If Ross's productivity advantage widens further, this becomes a visible investor comparison point in TJX earnings calls.

4

Gap improved operating margin +600bp in 24 months (FY2022 to FY2024) — a pace TJX has never achieved in any comparable period. Gap's turnaround was driven by markdown discipline, freight simplification, and inventory management — the same three mechanisms in P1, P3, and P4. Gap is not a flattering peer for TJX on most metrics, but it is the most direct proof that operational discipline drives margin step-change in US retail at scale.

5

TJX International segment (7.3% operating margin, FY2026) operates at less than half the Marmaxx benchmark (14.4%). International is not a small part of the business — it represents approximately $7.6B revenue (FY2026). The 7.1pp gap to Marmaxx on $7.6B of revenue = approximately $540M in annual operating income that would be released if International converged to Marmaxx-equivalent efficiency. This is a 10+ year gap, not a 2-year fix.

How We Built This

A 7-phase autonomous pipeline that converts public filings into a pursuit-ready executive briefing.

What This Is

  • ✓ An outside-in, fact-based hypothesis document built entirely from public sources
  • ✓ A structured starting position for a discovery conversation — not a final conclusion
  • ✓ A claim-tagged document where every number shows its source and confidence level
  • ✓ A commercial asset designed to earn the right to sell a 20-week proof-of-value engagement
  • ✓ Phase One validates or invalidates every hypothesis before any Phase Two commitment

What This Is Not

  • ✗ A replacement for proper discovery — internal data will confirm or disprove these hypotheses
  • ✗ A final recommendation — sizing estimates are directional, not audit-quality
  • ✗ A technology assessment — no proprietary system data was accessed
  • ✗ Guaranteed — if provocations are wrong, Phase One says so and the engagement stops

7 Specialist Agents

The only agent with web search access. Collects SEC filings, earnings transcripts, investor presentations, tech disclosures, peer data, and current signals. Runs a source quality gate — workflow stops if mandatory sources are missing.

Agent 2 — Company Analyst

Phase 2: Research

Analyzes collected data into structured company snapshot, management roadmap, and sector context. No web search — works exclusively from Phase 1 outputs.

Agent 3 — Peer Benchmarker

Phase 3: Benchmark

Constructs peer set and builds full P&L benchmarking table. Normalizes metrics across different fiscal year ends and accounting conventions. Flags non-comparable figures.

Agent 4 — Operating Model Analyst

Phase 4: Deep Analysis

Two sub-sections: (A) technology and operations footprint, transformation capacity, body-vs-brain diagnosis; (B) value levers, AI value stream ranking, moat analysis. Firewall between A and B prevents circular reasoning.

Phases 6–7

Phase 6 produces dual-track markdown (Track A client-facing, Track B internal). Phase 7 renders this HTML file — Tailwind CSS, Plotly charts, three tabs, print CSS. No AI/agent language in Track A.
