## PEER SET OVERVIEW

TJX's peer set spans two distinct competitive clusters:
1. **Direct off-price peers** — same business model, compete for the same shoppers and vendor relationships: Ross Stores, Burlington Stores
2. **Value/affordable apparel peers** — different model but compete for the same customer wallet: H&M, Inditex/Zara, Next plc, Primark
3. **Indirect / department store peers** — Nordstrom Rack (off-price arm of JWN), Gap Inc.

## DETAILED PEER PROFILES

### 1. ROSS STORES (ROST)
**Business:** Off-price apparel, accessories, home furnishings. US-only (Ross Dress for Less, dd's DISCOUNTS). ~1,756 stores.
**Model similarity to TJX:** Very high — nearly identical off-price buying model, no e-commerce for Ross Dress for Less

| Metric | FY2023 | FY2024 | FY2025 |
|---|---|---|---|
| Revenue | ~$18.7B | $20.4B | **$21.1B** |
| Gross Margin | ~26.3% | ~26.8% | **27.8%** |
| Operating Margin | ~10.3% | 11.3% | **12.2%** |
| Net Income | ~$1.5B | $1.875B | $2.091B |
| Diluted EPS | ~$1.50 | ~$2.00 | ~$2.25 |

**Key differences vs. TJX:**
- US-only vs. TJX's 9-country footprint
- No e-commerce (deliberately)
- Fewer SKUs, simpler assortment
- Higher operating margin than TJX in recent years [FACT]
- Investing $1.1B in CapEx FY2026 including DC automation [FACT]

Source: https://investors.rossstores.com/news-releases/news-release-details/ross-stores-reports-results-fourth-quarter-and-fiscal-2024

### 4. INDITEX / ZARA (BME: ITX)
**Business:** Multi-format fast fashion. Brands: Zara, Pull&Bear, Massimo Dutti, Bershka, Stradivarius, Oysho, Zara Home, Uterqüe. ~5,700 stores + strong online. Spanish company.
**Model similarity to TJX:** Low — full-price, design-led, ultra-fast supply chain. But a key competitor for premium value fashion customers.

| Metric | FY2022 | FY2023 | FY2024 |
|---|---|---|---|
| Revenue | €32,569M | **€35,947M** | **€38,612M** |
| Gross Margin | ~57.4% | 57.8% | 57.8% |
| EBIT Margin | ~17% | ~18.8% | ~19.7% |
| Net Income | €4,130M | €5,400M | **€5,908M** |

**Key differences vs. TJX:**
- Structurally superior margins (57.8% gross vs. TJX 31%)
- Owns design-to-shelf cycle; ~2-3 week lead time for trending items
- E-commerce is ~30% of sales (~€12B online)
- Premium brand positioning with aspirational consumer
- Massive technology investment in RFID, supply chain, data analytics

### Key Observations:
1. TJX operating margin (11.2%) is below Ross (12.2%) despite 2.7x the revenue — suggesting operational scale not fully captured [FACT/INFERENCE]
2. TJX gross margin (30.6%) is structurally lower than H&M (53.4%) and Inditex (57.8%) — reflecting cost-of-goods-sold structure of off-price vs. full-price models [FACT]
3. Inditex operates at ~19.7% EBIT margin on €38.6B revenue — the global efficiency benchmark [FACT]
4. TJX's e-commerce exposure (~1-3%) is the lowest among major comparables — significant digital capability gap vs. peers [INFERENCE]
5. Burlington is aggressively automating DCs while TJX has not made equivalent public commitments [FACT for Burlington; INFERENCE for TJX comparison]
