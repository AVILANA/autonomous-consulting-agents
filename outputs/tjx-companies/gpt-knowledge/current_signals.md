## SIGNAL 4: TARIFF DYNAMICS — HEADWIND EXPECTED BUT MANAGED (Throughout FY2026)
**Dates:** Multiple, throughout 2025 and early 2026
**Sources:** 
- https://www.supplychaindive.com/news/tjx-tariffs-inventory-buying-opportunities-q3-earns-china/733822/
- https://www.retaildive.com/news/tariffs-squeeze-off-price-retailers/749177/
- https://www.cnbc.com/2025/08/20/tjx-cos-tjx-q2-2026-earnings.html
**Type:** Macro Risk / Competitive Dynamics

**Key facts:**
- Analyst estimate: ~40% of TJX U.S. containers from China (direct + indirect) [FACT — analyst estimate, not company confirmed]
- TJX disclosed: <10% of US inventory sourced directly from China [FACT per 10-K]
- Bank of America lowered EPS estimates for 2026-2027 due to tariff pressure [FACT]
- TJX Q2 FY26 (August 2025): Beat earnings despite tariff pressure; raised guidance [FACT]
- CEO Herrman: "laser focused on our initiatives to offset them by remaining flexible and executing our opportunistic buying approach" [FACT]
- TJX advantage: buys after goods are already imported (does not manufacture direct from China) [FACT]
- Tariffs create vendor panic → excess inventory → buying opportunities for TJX [FACT per management]

**Signal interpretation:** [INFERENCE] The tariff narrative is actually a net positive signal for TJX's medium-term outlook. Unlike traditional retailers who source direct from Chinese manufacturers, TJX's opportunistic model means it captures the liquidation surplus that tariff-panicked brands create. The short-term gross margin pressure (tariff-inflated COGS for branded goods entering TJX's pipeline) is manageable; the long-term inventory availability windfall is significant.

## SIGNAL 9: MARGIN EXPANSION TRAJECTORY — STRUCTURAL IMPROVEMENT (FY2023–FY2026)
**Date:** Multi-year pattern identified in FY2026 reporting
**Source:** Earnings releases FY2023–FY2026
**Type:** Financial Performance Pattern

**Key facts:**
- Gross margin improved from 27.6% (FY2023 trough) to 31.0% (FY2026) = +340bps in 3 years [FACT]
- Operating margin improved from 9.3% (FY2023) to 11.9% (FY2026) = +260bps in 3 years [FACT]
- Lower inventory shrink specifically cited as margin driver FY2024 [FACT]
- Lower freight costs cited as margin driver FY2024–FY2025 [FACT]
- Lower markdowns and better markon cited as drivers [FACT]

**Signal interpretation:** [INFERENCE] The margin expansion is impressive but has multiple components. Some are structural (better buying, fewer markdowns = model confidence), some are cyclical (freight normalization, shrink improvements). Sustaining 31%+ gross margin into FY2027+ requires continued excellence in buying. If vendor supply remains abundant (tariff tailwind), margins could expand further.
