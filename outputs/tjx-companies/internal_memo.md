## 12. COMPETITIVE INTELLIGENCE — FIVE KEY TAKEAWAYS

1. **Burlington's Georgia DC opens in 2026 — this is TJX's competitive clock.** Burlington's "highly automated" Savannah DC will publish its first operating metrics within 12–18 months. When it does, analysts will benchmark TJX's three new DCs against it. TJX currently has no automation announcement to counter with. The window for Accenture to lead the DC automation conversation is now — before Burlington's results make it a defensive response for TJX.

2. **Ross Stores generates 47% more revenue per employee than TJX on the same business model.** At $240K revenue per employee (Ross) versus $163K (TJX), the gap is the most directly threatening peer comparison in the set. Some of this is structural (TJX's higher receiving complexity), but analyst framing will attribute it to TJX's absent operational technology program. This is the CFO and activist investor story — it needs an answer.

3. **Inditex at 19.7% EBIT margin kills the "European retail is structurally expensive" counterargument.** TJX International at 7.3% in the same EU markets that Inditex operates at 19.7% confirms that the structural cost argument explains at most 5–6pp of the 12pp Inditex-to-TJX-International gap. The rest is operational. This is the single most powerful peer data point for P4.

4. **H&M's 170M loyalty members create a compounding information advantage TJX cannot buy.** H&M's demand forecasting algorithms train on individual purchase history at category and price point level. TJX's 1,400 buyers make decisions without any individual-level signal. This is not an argument for TJX to launch a loyalty program (management has rejected that model). It is an argument for TJX to build the AI buyer intelligence layer that extracts equivalent signal from its own 5,214-store sell-through data — which exists and is proprietary.

5. **Gap Inc.'s +600bp operating margin improvement in 2 years is the playbook precedent TJX's management needs to see.** Gap went from ~1% operating margin (FY2022) to ~7% (FY2024) through systematic store operational discipline. This is not a financial engineering story — it is an operational execution story. For a COO-audience conversation, Gap's turnaround pace is more compelling than Inditex's structural superiority. It answers the implicit question: "how fast can we actually move?"
