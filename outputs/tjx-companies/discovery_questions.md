# TJX Companies — Discovery Questions
## Phase 5 Job 4: Clean Questions Earned by Each Provocation
## April 2026 | No web search used.

These are the questions our provocations earn us the right to ask. Each question is specific, answerable with internal data, and designed to convert our external hypothesis into a confirmed baseline for Phase One validation. These are the questions we ask in the first working session after the client agrees to engage.

---

## FROM PROVOCATION 1 — DC AUTOMATION DESIGN

**Primary question:**
What are the current construction design specifications for WMS (Warehouse Management System) infrastructure, dock scheduling technology, and automation equipment in the NJ, El Paso TX, and Ohio DC projects? Has a final decision been made on automation design, or is that decision still open?

**Secondary questions:**
1. Who is the executive decision-maker for DC construction specifications? Is supply chain technology represented in those decisions alongside capital projects and facilities?
2. What is the current DC receiving cycle time — from vendor truck arrival at the dock to lot being store-ready for dispatch? How does this vary across DC age and format?
3. What is TJX's DC labor cost per unit received and processed, across the network? How does this compare across the oldest and newest DCs?
4. Does any existing TJX DC operate any form of WMS, automated sortation, or dock management technology — even a partial or legacy deployment?
5. When Burlington's highly automated Georgia DC publishes its first operating results (expected 2027), what is TJX's plan to respond to analyst comparisons?

**What these questions validate:** The title claim ("still being designed" + Burlington/Ross automation investments). The cost of inaction (retrofit vs. design-in economics). The 12-month decision window urgency.

## FROM PROVOCATION 2 — BUYER INTELLIGENCE

**Primary question:**
What is the distribution of markon (markup above cost) achieved across TJX's 1,400 buyers within the same product category and season? Is buyer performance variance tracked systematically, and if so, what drives the gap between top-quartile and bottom-quartile buyer outcomes?

**Secondary questions:**
1. What is the average deal evaluation time from vendor contact to commitment decision? Where does time get lost in that cycle — vendor outreach, internal approval, pricing analysis, or DC capacity confirmation?
2. Is there a systematic feedback loop from store sell-through data back to individual buyer decisions? When a buyer's lot sells through in 2 weeks vs. 6 weeks, does that outcome get recorded against that buyer's performance and used to calibrate future decisions?
3. What deal data currently sits in Oracle EBS (Enterprise Resource Planning) by vendor, category, price paid, markon achieved, and sell-through outcome? Is this data used for anything beyond financial reporting?
4. What percentage of deals coming through the pipeline right now (at the current historic volume peak) are being passed rather than accepted? Is bandwidth the constraint, or is pricing the constraint?
5. How is the India GCC (Global Capability Center) Data & Automation Solutions service line currently being used — what specific data or analytics projects is it running for the buying organization?

**What these questions validate:** The buying intelligence gap. The data foundation available for AI model training. The buyer productivity opportunity. The India GCC as a realistic delivery vehicle.

## FROM PROVOCATION 3 — FREIGHT AUDIT

**Primary question:**
Does TJX have any freight invoice reconciliation process, whether internal, outsourced, or third-party contingency? What percentage of total freight invoice volume — across all regions, carrier types (ocean, air, domestic trucking, last mile), and currencies — is currently reviewed for billing accuracy?

**Secondary questions:**
1. What is TJX's total annual freight spend? How is it split between inbound (vendor-to-DC) and outbound (DC-to-store)? What share of inbound freight is vendor-managed vs. TJX-controlled?
2. What is TJX's current freight overcharge recovery rate, if tracked? When was the last time a systematic audit of carrier invoices was conducted across all geographies?
3. Does TJX have any form of centralized TMS (Transportation Management System) for any segment of its carrier network — US domestic, ocean, or European cross-border?
4. How are carrier contracts negotiated? At the segment level (Marmaxx separately from HomeGoods, or US separately from Europe), or is there a global carrier portfolio view? How often are contracts competitively bid?
5. What is the process for handling carrier invoice disputes today? Who owns that process — AP (Accounts Payable), logistics, or procurement — and what is the average resolution time for a disputed invoice?

**What these questions validate:** Whether a freight audit already exists (repositions the provocation to coverage depth vs. existence). The total freight spend baseline needed to calculate the dollar opportunity. The audit program scope and coverage gaps.

## FROM PROVOCATION 4 — ALLOCATION INTELLIGENCE

**Primary question:**
What is TJX's markdown rate for stores in their first 2 years of operation vs. the network average? How are initial allocations set for brand-new stores in new markets — what is the methodology for a TK Maxx opening in a new Spanish city or a new HomeSense in a suburban US market?

**Secondary questions:**
1. How long does it typically take for a new store's allocation profile to converge to its analogue cluster average — is it 1 season, 2 seasons, or longer?
2. Has Oracle Retail Allocation been customized for TJX's non-SKU model? Is there any AI or machine learning layer on top of Oracle Retail Allocation that addresses category absorption rate prediction without SKU-level history?
3. What is the inter-DC transfer rate — the percentage of lots that need to be moved from one DC to another after initial allocation? Does this rate differ significantly for new stores vs. mature stores?
4. What data is currently available on store-level category absorption rates — how fast does each store cluster sell off women's apparel lots vs. home goods vs. footwear, by demographic zone?
5. Lower markdowns drove a confirmed gross margin improvement in FY2024 and FY2025. Can you identify what specific process changes — in buying, allocation, or store operations — drove that improvement? Which of those changes are sustaining into FY2026 and FY2027?

**What these questions validate:** The new-store cold-start allocation gap. Whether the current analogue matching methodology is adequate or leaves measurable markdown on the table. The margin sustainability question as 146 stores open per year.

## FROM PROVOCATION 5 — SYNTHESIS

**Primary question:**
What is TJX's internal operating margin target for FY2028? Is there a board-level or management commitment to improving consolidated margin from 11.9% (FY2026) toward the Marmaxx benchmark of 14.4%? Or is the International drag treated as a permanent structural discount to the US business performance?

**Secondary questions:**
1. What is the combined operating margin target, if any, in the FY2027 annual operating plan? Does management model a path from 11.9% to 12%+ through supply chain operational improvement?
2. Of the four operational gaps identified (DC automation design, buyer intelligence, freight audit, allocation intelligence) — which, if any, are already in the FY2027 or FY2028 capital plan or technology roadmap?
3. What is TJX's current view on the combined value of operational technology investment relative to the ongoing store expansion program? Is there an internal analysis comparing the ROI of opening a new store vs. improving the operational efficiency of 5,214 existing stores?
4. When Ross Stores publishes its AI supply chain investment results and Burlington publishes its automated DC results in 2027 — how does TJX's board expect to respond to those comparisons in earnings calls?
5. At 7,000 stores (TJX's stated long-term target), what is the internal operating margin assumption? Is the current 11.9% sustainable at that scale without the supply chain infrastructure investments described in Provocations 1–4?

**What these questions validate:** The strategic framing for Phase One. Whether management has already identified these gaps internally. The scale of the EBIT opportunity relative to management commitments. The urgency created by peer investments publishing results.

## QUESTION DESIGN PRINCIPLES

Each question above was designed to meet three criteria:

1. **Answerable with internal data.** Every question can be answered by pulling a specific dataset, report, or system output that TJX has internally. No question requires speculation or external research.

2. **Converts hypothesis to baseline.** Each question turns our outside-in INFERENCE into a confirmed starting number for Phase One. The question is not "are we right?" — the question is "what does the actual number tell us, and does it confirm or revise our estimate?"

3. **Earns the right to the next conversation.** Each question, answered honestly, moves the conversation from "here is what we see from the outside" to "here is what we now know from the inside — and here is the revised opportunity." This is the Phase One deliverable: a confirmed baseline that determines whether Phase Two scope is warranted and what the measurable outcome target should be.
