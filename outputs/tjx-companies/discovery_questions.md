# TJX Companies — Discovery Questions
## Phase 5 Job 4: Clean Questions Earned by Each Provocation
## April 2026 (Revised — tech_ops_raw.md corrections applied) | No web search used.

These are the questions our provocations earn us the right to ask. Each question is specific, answerable with internal data, and designed to convert our outside-in hypothesis into a confirmed baseline for Phase One. These are asked in the first working session after the client agrees to engage.

---

## FROM PROVOCATION 1 — DC AUTOMATION DESIGN

**Primary question:**
What are the current construction design specifications for WMS (Warehouse Management System) infrastructure, dock automation, sortation systems, and computer vision receiving capability in the NJ, El Paso TX, Sunbridge FL, and Ohio DCs currently under construction? Has a final decision been made on automation hardware, or is that decision still open?

**Secondary questions:**
1. The Manhattan WMS active upgrade evaluation (November 2025) — is its scope limited to a software/cloud migration, or does it include automation hardware integration design for the four new facilities? Who is the decision-maker for that scope question?
2. What is the current DC receiving cycle time — from vendor truck arrival at the dock to lot being store-ready for dispatch? How does this vary across TJX's oldest DCs vs. the UK DCs equipped with Dematic automation since 2008?
3. What is TJX's DC labor cost per unit received and processed across the US network? How does this compare across the oldest and newest US facilities?
4. Burlington's highly automated Georgia DC will publish its first operating results in 2027. What is TJX's internal benchmark response — is there a stated goal for DC throughput efficiency that this peer data would be compared against?
5. Who are the executive sponsors for the four new DC construction projects? Is supply chain technology (WMS architecture, automation hardware) a separate workstream from capital projects and facilities — or integrated into the same decision process?

## FROM PROVOCATION 2 — BUYER INTELLIGENCE

**Primary question:**
What is the current variance in markon (markup above cost) achieved across TJX's 1,400 buyers within the same category, vendor tier, and season — and is that variance tracked systematically?

**Secondary questions:**
1. What percentage of incoming vendor deal flow is evaluated by a buyer and accepted or rejected, vs. declined without evaluation due to buyer bandwidth? Is there a queue of deals waiting for buyer attention during the current peak deal flow period?
2. Does any buyer today use a structured deal evaluation framework — scoring tools, historical comparison, vendor performance tracking — or is deal evaluation entirely relationship-driven and judgment-based?
3. What data from Oracle EBS (deal history: vendor, category, price paid, allocation, sell-through, markdown outcome) is currently available to buyers at the time of a deal evaluation decision? Is historical deal outcome data surfaced in a buyer-facing dashboard, or is it housed in the ERP system and not practically accessible during negotiations?
4. How does the India GCC (Global Capability Center) Data & Automation Solutions team currently interact with the buying organization? Is there an active project connecting GCC data capability to merchandise buying, or are these separate operations today?
5. When the tariff buying windfall normalizes (estimated 2–3 year horizon), what is TJX's internal view on whether gross margin can be sustained above 31% through buying excellence alone — and what operational improvements are planned to support that target?

## FROM PROVOCATION 3 — FREIGHT INVOICE AUTOMATION

**Primary question:**
What percentage of TJX US freight invoices are currently verified against contracted lane rates via automated rate card matching — and what is the annual total freight overcharge recovery across the domestic and international carrier network?

**Secondary questions:**
1. The Freight Payment Coordinator role at Marlborough MA authorizes $700M+ in domestic and international logistics payments annually. What is TJX's total freight spend across all modes (Truckload, Intermodal, LTL, parcel, ocean) and all geographies (US domestic, transoceanic inbound, European outbound) — is $700M the floor for a subset, or an estimate of total?
2. Does the TMS (Transportation Management System) currently perform automated rate card matching (comparing each invoice against the contracted lane rate, fuel surcharge formula, and accessorial charge schedule) — or does the Freight Payment Coordinator team perform this matching manually via exception-based sampling?
3. TJX Europe's logistics are managed by DHL with an integrated European Transport Control Tower. How does TJX US's freight audit process compare to what DHL provides contractually for Europe — and is there any intent to bring comparable systematic oversight to TJX US freight?
4. What is TJX's routing guide compliance rate — the percentage of carrier shipments where the carrier applies the contracted lane rate vs. a higher applicable rate? Is this measured systematically across all carriers and lanes?
5. Has TJX evaluated any freight audit software platforms (automated invoice matching, duplicate detection, multi-currency overbilling detection) — and if so, what was the evaluation outcome?

## FROM PROVOCATION 4 — NEW STORE ALLOCATION ACCURACY

**Primary question:**
What is TJX's current markdown rate in the first 2 seasons of a new store's operation vs. the network average for comparable stores in the same banner and geographic region?

**Secondary questions:**
1. How are initial allocation profiles built for new stores today — specifically for first-time markets like Spain (FY2027 opening, 5 stores) and new Australian locations, where no historical TJX demand pattern exists? What is the analogue matching process, and how is analogue quality validated?
2. What is the current methodology for deciding how much of each lot category (women's apparel, menswear, home goods, footwear) goes to a new store vs. an established store in the same DC catchment zone? Is this a rule-based decision or a buyer judgment call?
3. How long does it take for a new store's allocation profile to "converge" to a stable pattern — the point at which the allocation model treats the new store equivalently to a mature store? Is this measured?
4. Oracle Retail Allocation is confirmed as the allocation tool. Has TJX explored ML-based demand profile bootstrapping (building a new store's initial profile from demographic, geographic, and co-tenant data rather than just analogue store matching) — either within Oracle's product roadmap or as a custom development?
5. The 340bp gross margin improvement (FY2023–FY2026) was partially attributed to lower markdowns. Is that markdown improvement uniformly distributed across all stores including recent openings, or is it concentrated in the mature store base — meaning new stores may not yet have captured the same improvement?

## FROM PROVOCATION 5 — SYNTHESIS

**Primary question:**
Of the four value pools — DC throughput improvement, buyer deal quality improvement, freight invoice recovery, and new-store allocation accuracy — which are already tracked with internal baselines today, and which would require a Phase One diagnostic to confirm the gap and size the opportunity?

**Secondary questions:**
1. TJX's Marmaxx segment runs at 14.4% operating margin (FY2026) while the consolidated total is 11.9%. Has management quantified the operational efficiency gap between Marmaxx and TJX International as a target — i.e., is there an internal roadmap for International margin improvement that operational investments are meant to support?
2. Ross Stores generates higher operating margin (12.2%, FY2025) at 2.7x less scale on the same off-price model. Has TJX's management analyzed the drivers of Ross's operating efficiency advantage — and is DC automation investment or buyer productivity a named factor in that analysis?
3. The FY2027 CapEx of $2.2B–$2.3B covers stores, remodels, DC expansion, and infrastructure. What is the technology investment allocation within that CapEx — is there a budget line for supply chain technology (WMS upgrade, freight systems, allocation tools) that sits within or alongside the DC construction budget?
4. TJX's India GCC is actively building a Logistics Center of Excellence (confirmed by CLO Raina Avalon). What is the current scope and timeline for that CoE — and is buyer intelligence, freight analytics, or allocation optimization within its mandate?
5. For a Phase One engagement: which of the four operational gaps would TJX's team prioritize as the most urgent to validate — and is there an executive sponsor with the authority to provide internal data access for a 4-week baseline diagnostic?
