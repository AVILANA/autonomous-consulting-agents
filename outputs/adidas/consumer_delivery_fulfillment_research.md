**Methodology:** Web search across press releases, official announcements, case studies, job postings, and vendor references.
**Confidence Ratings:** CONFIRMED = press release or official announcement | LIKELY = multiple indirect signals | UNCONFIRMED = single or weak signal

| Standard | 3–7 business days (typically 3–5 in major metros) | $5 for non-members; free for adiClub members | Official adidas.com help page |

## SECTION 2: FULFILLMENT MODELS

### 2.1 Ship-from-Store

**Rating: LIKELY**

Evidence signals:
- GK Software's OmniPOS platform, deployed across more than 1,300 adidas stores globally, explicitly enables "ship-from-store" as a capability alongside click-and-collect. GK and adidas won Gold at the 2024 Convrt Awards for "Customer Experience Initiative."
- adidas's RFID rollout (achieving >99% stock accuracy across stores) is the foundational data layer enabling reliable ship-from-store without phantom inventory errors.
- GK blog (September 2024) describes "unified inventory visibility underpinning services such as click and collect and ship-from-store."

**No press release explicitly announcing "adidas launches ship-from-store at scale."** The capability is confirmed at the technology layer but not publicly quantified (% of orders, number of stores active, etc.).

### 2.2 Dark Stores / Microfulfillment Centers

**Rating: UNCONFIRMED**

No evidence found of adidas operating dedicated dark stores or urban microfulfillment centers. adidas's fulfillment is structured around large regional DCs (see Section 3). The 2019 Zalando Paris pilot used Zalando's own logistics center, not an adidas dark store.

### 2.3 Click & Collect (BOPIS)

**Rating: CONFIRMED**

- adidas.co.uk has a dedicated "Click & Collect" help section, with 0–5 day collection windows at adidas stores.
- GK OmniPOS explicitly supports click-and-collect at the POS layer.
- adidas.com (US) offers "collect in-store" as a delivery option.
- 1,933 adidas-owned stores globally (838 concept stores + 1,095 factory outlets) as of 2024 form the pick-up network.

### 2.4 Fulfillment Mix: DC vs. Store

**Rating: INFERENCE**

adidas does not publicly disclose the split of online orders fulfilled from DCs vs. stores. Based on available signals:
- Primary online order fulfillment appears to be DC-led (Wilkes-Barre PA for US; Mantova/Moissy for Europe; Suzhou for China).
- Store-based fulfillment (ship-from-store) is an available capability but likely a secondary/overflow channel, not the primary mode.
- The February 2025 Amazon Buy with Prime deal routes eligible adidas.com orders through Amazon's MCF (Multi-Channel Fulfillment) network — a third structural layer added to adidas's US fulfillment mix.

## SECTION 3: DISTRIBUTION CENTER AUTOMATION AND THROUGHPUT

### 3.1 Wilkes-Barre / Hanover Township, Pennsylvania (US)

- **Size:** 843,000 sq ft (~78,000 sqm)
- **Opened:** June 2019
- **Capacity:** Up to 200,000 units/day (peak); SKUs: 71,000+; racking: up to 16 million cases
- **Speed:** Orders packed within 2 hours of purchase
- **Delivery reach:** Same-day to select NE locations; 1-day ground to Northeast US; 2-day ground nationwide
- **Purpose:** Primary US DTC e-commerce and North American wholesale
- **Automation provider:** Interlake Mecalux (racking/storage system confirmed via case study)
- **WMS:** Manhattan Associates (confirmed via Dematic/Manhattan integration references)
- **Rating: CONFIRMED** — Interlake Mecalux case study; Dematic case study

### 3.2 Spartanburg, South Carolina (US)

- **Campus:** 258-acre campus
- **Automation:** Honeywell Intelligrated designed and installed automated conveyor and sortation systems across two DCs on campus
- **Purpose:** Wholesale + retail replenishment; conveyor/sortation implies B2B fulfillment orientation
- **Hiring:** Actively hiring in early 2025 (300+ new positions announced)
- **Rating: CONFIRMED** — Honeywell Intelligrated case study (official PDF)

### 3.3 Mantova (Mantua), Italy — European Super Hub

- **Operator:** Kuehne+Nagel
- **Investment:** €350 million (Kuehne+Nagel's single largest investment to date)
- **Size:** 130,000 sqm
- **Opened:** September 2024
- **Throughput:** Up to 375,000 parcels/day (standard); up to 500,000 shipments/day (peak)
- **Technology:** 700+ robots; 12 miles (~20 km) of conveyor belts; 675 automatic shuttles; LEED Gold certified
- **Service area:** 19 countries in Southern and Eastern Europe (e-commerce, retail, and wholesale)
- **Rating: CONFIRMED** — Kuehne+Nagel press release; multiple logistics trade publications

### 3.4 Suzhou, China

- **Partner:** Geek+ (robotics)
- **Opened:** September 21, 2023
- **Size:** 139,000 sqm
- **Throughput:** More than 1 million pieces/day
- **Capacity:** 10 million pieces of apparel and footwear in storage
- **Technology:** Geek+ mobile robots (AMRs); customized item location detection; human-machine collaboration
- **Purpose:** China and Asia-Pacific distribution (apparel and footwear)
- **Rating: CONFIRMED** — Joint Geek+/adidas press release (PR Newswire, September 2023)

### 3.5 Manchester / Trafford, UK

- **Size:** 350,000 sq ft (~32,500 sqm)
- **Technology:** Dematic — large automated pallet and tote ASRS + goods-to-person (GTP) picking system; Dematic iQ software integrated with Manhattan Associates WMS
- **Throughput:** 40,000+ split case items/day; peak pick rate 1,500 units/hour; average 500 units/hour
- **Purpose:** UK, Ireland, and Benelux retail and wholesale
- **Investment:** £20 million automation
- **Rating: CONFIRMED** — Dematic case study

### 3.6 Brazil — Extrema, Minas Gerais

- **Operator:** DHL Supply Chain
- **Opened:** February 2024
- **Size:** ~40,000 sqm
- **Investment:** R$70 million+
- **Technology:** Advanced cloud WMS; automatic conveyors; automated sorting; robot in e-commerce area
- **Purpose:** E-commerce, retail, and own stores across Brazil
- **Rating: CONFIRMED** — DHL press release February 2024

### 3.7 Consumer (DTC) vs. Wholesale Automation Split

**[INFERENCE]:** The Wilkes-Barre PA facility is the clearest DTC-dedicated DC in adidas's US network — built specifically for e-commerce. Spartanburg is more mixed (wholesale + retail replenishment based on sortation system design). The Mantova Italy hub explicitly serves all three channels (e-commerce + retail + wholesale). Suzhou China appears to be a combined facility. adidas has not published a formal DC network map showing channel-specific routing logic.

## SECTION 6: KEY STRATEGIC OBSERVATIONS

### 6.1 Channel Strategy Shift [CONFIRMED]
adidas reversed its aggressive DTC-first push under CEO Björn Gulden (from 2023), moving to a balanced hybrid model. In 2024: wholesale grew 14%, DTC grew 11%, e-commerce grew 6% (18% ex-Yeezy). Wholesale remains 60% of net sales. The original "50% DTC by 2025" target was not achieved.

### 6.2 Fulfillment Speed Gap vs. Amazon [CONFIRMED + INFERENCE]
adidas's direct-to-consumer fulfillment is competitive (2-day nationwide from Wilkes-Barre) but cannot match Amazon Prime speed at scale. The February 2025 "Buy with Prime" partnership is a structural admission of this gap — adidas is outsourcing speed-sensitive fulfillment to Amazon's MCF network rather than building its own same-day capability.

### 6.3 DC Network is Wholesale-First, DTC-Capable [INFERENCE]
adidas's DC network was built for wholesale efficiency (bulk replenishment to wholesale accounts). DTC e-commerce capability has been layered in. The Wilkes-Barre PA DC is the clearest purpose-built DTC facility. The Mantova and Suzhou super-hubs serve all channels. No evidence of a pure DTC micro-fulfillment network.

### 6.4 TRANS4RM (SAP S/4HANA on AWS) is the Core Technology Transformation [CONFIRMED]
This is adidas's largest-ever IT program. Central Finance went live November 2023. Full Finance and Purchasing targeted for 2024. Supply chain management and sales are subsequent waves. The program creates the foundational data architecture for real-time inventory visibility, demand sensing, and omnichannel fulfillment orchestration.

### 6.5 Technology Stack is SAP-Centric, Not Best-of-Breed [INFERENCE]
- ERP: SAP S/4HANA (not Oracle)
- Planning: o9 Solutions (not Blue Yonder or Kinaxis)
- ITSM: ServiceNow (not BMC)
- WMS: Manhattan Associates (established; may coexist with SAP EWM in new S/4 landscape)
- HCM: SAP SuccessFactors (not Workday)
- Procurement: Coupa (confirmed for Latin America; global scope unclear)
- Process automation: SAP BTP (not UiPath as primary RPA vehicle)
