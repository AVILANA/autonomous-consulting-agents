## Revenue by Segment / Geography — Available Data

- Adidas brand: +13% CN
- Double-digit growth in: Europe, Greater China, Latin America
- Footwear: +17%
- Q4 2024 revenue: €5,965M (+24% YoY)

### FY 2025 (Source: FY2025 Press Release)
- Total revenue: €24,811M (record; +4.8% YoY in EUR terms; +13% CN excl. Yeezy prior year)
- Adidas brand CN growth: +13% (9 consecutive quarters of double-digit growth)
- All markets and channels: double-digit CN growth
- Operating profit margin: 8.3% (+2.6pp YoY)
- Dividend increase: +40%
- Share buyback: launched

### Q3 2024 (Source: Q3 2024 Press Release)
- Revenue: €6,438M (CN +10.3%, +14% excl. Yeezy)
- Gross margin: 51.3%
- EBIT: €598M (+~50% YoY)
- EBIT margin: 9.3%

### Q4 2024 (Source: Q4 2024 Press Release)
- Revenue: €5,965M (+24% YoY in EUR)
- EBIT: €57M (vs. loss of €377M prior year)

## Key Contextual Events Affecting Financials

| Year | Event | Financial Impact |
|------|-------|----------------|
| 2020 | COVID-19 pandemic | Revenue -16%, net income -78%, e-commerce +53% |
| 2021 | Recovery + Reebok sale (Feb 2022) | Revenue +7%, net income inflated by Reebok disposal gain |
| 2022 | Russia/Ukraine war (exit), China COVID lockdowns, Kanye West/Yeezy relationship ended Oct 2022 | Revenue +6% but EBIT collapsed -66%; massive inventory build (€5,973M) |
| 2023 | Yeezy wind-down (~€1.0B revenue loss), inventory destocking, restructuring | Revenue -5%, EBIT -60% to €268M |
| 2024 | Yeezy inventory sold (~€650M revenue), strong brand recovery, Bjørn Gulden turnaround continues | Revenue +11%, EBIT +399%, gross margin recovery to 50.8% |
| 2025 | Record year, all markets double-digit CN growth, no Yeezy tailwind | Revenue +4.8% (record €24.8B), EBIT +54%, 40% dividend hike, buyback |
