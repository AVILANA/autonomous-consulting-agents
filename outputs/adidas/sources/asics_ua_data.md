## ASICS Corporation (TSE: 7936 / OTC: ASCCY)

**Fiscal year ends:** December 31
**Reporting currency:** Japanese Yen (¥M = millions of yen)
**Accounting standard:** Japan GAAP

> Note: ASICS reports "Selling, General and Administrative Expenses" (SGA) as a single combined line item below gross profit. Japan GAAP P&L format: Net Sales → Cost of Sales → Gross Profit → SGA → Operating Profit.

**FY2022 notes:**
- [FACT] Net sales ¥484,600M and operating income ¥34,000M (7.0% margin) confirmed from FY2022 Consolidated Financial Summary (fiscal year ended December 31, 2022).
- [FACT] Gross margin 49.7% confirmed; improvement attributable to better sales channel mix and optimized selling prices, partly offset by FX headwinds on purchasing costs and higher North America/Europe logistics costs.
- [FACT] Operating income hit record high at the time, +54.9% YoY; achieved Mid-term Plan 2023 ¥25B target ahead of schedule.
- [INFERENCE] Gross profit (~¥240.8B) and SGA (~¥206.8B) derived arithmetically: net sales × gross margin % and gross profit − operating profit. Cross-checked against confirmed FY2023/FY2024 SGA ratios (42–43%).

**FY2023 notes:**
- [FACT] All P&L figures confirmed from ASICS FY2023 Consolidated Financial Summary.
- [FACT] SGA ratio of 42.5% explicitly confirmed as "within the financial plan" per management commentary.
- Gross margin expanded +240 bps; driven by improving channel mix toward DTC and performance footwear categories.

**FY2024 notes:**
- [FACT] All P&L figures confirmed from official Summary of Consolidated Financial Statements, fiscal year ended December 31, 2024 (Japan GAAP).
- [FACT] Cost of Sales ¥299,648M and SGA ¥278,767M confirmed from official annual report income statement.
- Gross margin expanded further +370 bps to 55.8%; operating margin nearly doubled from 9.5% to 14.7%.
- [FACT] FY2024 was ASICS' highest-ever revenue, operating profit, and net income. Operating profit exceeded ¥100B for the first time in company history.
- [FACT] North America operating margin reached 8.3% (+7.0 pp YoY); ASICS Japan operating margin reached 23.5% (+12.7 pp).
- Revenue USD equivalent: ~$4.5B (FY2024) at prevailing exchange rates.

### ASICS — Key Analytical Observations

- [FACT] Revenue compounded at ~18–22% annually across FY2022–FY2024 — exceptional for a mature specialty footwear brand.
- [FACT] Gross margin expanded 610 bps over three years (49.7% → 55.8%), approaching Nike/premium-brand territory.
- [INFERENCE] Gross margin expansion has been the primary operating leverage driver; SGA ratio has declined only modestly (~42.7% → ~41.1%), suggesting limited fixed-cost operating leverage in SGA despite scale.
- [INFERENCE] ASICS' FY2024 operating margin of 14.7% significantly exceeds Adidas FY2024 (~5–6%) — a substantial peer gap on profitability.
- [ASSUMPTION] Revenue growth is heavily performance/running-driven; lifestyle segment momentum (FY2024: +50%+ per management commentary) adds further upside risk to gross margin via ASP mix.
- [ASSUMPTION] **Discovery question:** How much of gross margin expansion is attributable to (a) channel mix shift toward DTC vs. (b) pricing power vs. (c) input/logistics cost reduction?

**Reporting currency:** US Dollars ($M = millions of USD)
**Accounting standard:** US GAAP

**FY2023 notes:**
- [FACT] Revenue $5.9B (+3% YoY) from Q4 FY2023 earnings release (May 9, 2023) and SEC filing.
- [FACT] Gross margin 44.9% (-470 bps YoY); decline driven by higher freight/product costs, promotional activity, distributor mix, and adverse FX.
- [FACT] SGA ~$2.4B (-2% YoY) per earnings release.
- [INFERENCE] Gross profit and operating income derived arithmetically. GAAP operating margin may differ slightly due to additional restructuring line items.
- [ASSUMPTION] FY2023 capex ~$200M per Q3 FY2023 guidance; actual cash flow statement figure not confirmed via web search. **Discovery question:** Confirm actual FY2023 capex from audited 10-K.

**FY2024 notes:**
- [FACT] Revenue $5.702B (-3.4% from $5.9B) per FY2024 Q4 earnings press release and SEC filing.
- [FACT] Gross margin 46.1% (+130 bps) driven by lower freight and product input costs.
- [FACT] SGA $2.424B (+1% reported), or $2.344B adjusted excluding $80M litigation reserve. Per official FY2024 earnings release.
- [FACT] Operating margin ~4.0% per FY2024 earnings press release.
- [FACT] GAAP operating income ~$228M; includes $80M litigation reserve which depresses GAAP margin.
- [ASSUMPTION] CapEx guidance $210–230M for FY2024 per Q3 FY2024 update; actual audited capex not confirmed from web search. **Discovery question:** Confirm FY2024 actual capex from 10-K cash flow statement.

**FY2025 notes:**
- [FACT] Revenue ~$5.163B (-9.4% from $5.702B) from Q4 FY2025 earnings press release.
- [FACT] Gross margin 47.9% (+180 bps); continued improvement despite revenue contraction — reflects "Sell Less, Earn More" repositioning: reducing promotions and off-price channel dependency.
- [FACT] SGA $2.592B (+8% YoY); absolute increase driven by restructuring charges, transformation costs, and impairment despite lower revenue base. SGA% reached ~50%.
- [FACT] GAAP operating loss ~-$185M; largely restructuring/impairment-driven. Adjusted operating income $198M (~3.8% margin).
- [FACT] CapEx $168.7M for FY2025 confirmed from earnings release.
- [INFERENCE] Management characterized FY2025 as a strategic reset year. Revenue decline partly deliberate (exiting low-margin wholesale, reducing off-price channel) rather than purely demand-driven.
- [ASSUMPTION] Restructuring charges and impairment indicate significant operational transformation still ongoing; full benefit not visible until FY2026–27. **Discovery question:** What is the expected "run-rate" SGA post-restructuring, and at what revenue level does Under Armour become structurally profitable?

### Under Armour — Key Analytical Observations

- [FACT] Two consecutive years of deliberate revenue contraction: FY2024 -3.4%, FY2025 -9.4%.
- [FACT] Gross margin has improved for three consecutive years: 44.9% → 46.1% → 47.9%; structural improvement underway.
- [INFERENCE] The gross margin recovery trajectory confirms that pricing discipline and channel quality improvement are working. However, the SGA cost base has not been rightsized to the lower revenue level, creating operating losses.
- [INFERENCE] SGA as % of revenue surged from ~40.7% (FY2023) to ~50.2% (FY2025) — the sharpest cost efficiency deterioration in the peer set. This reflects both real restructuring costs and a legacy fixed-cost structure inherited from a $6B revenue base now running on $5.2B.
- [ASSUMPTION] Brand recovery is underway but financial inflection has not yet occurred. The "Sell Less, Earn More" strategy requires sustained gross margin expansion to offset SGA rigidity. **Discovery question:** What is the FY2026 pathway — does revenue stabilize at $5B+ or continue declining? At what point does adjusted operating margin return to 8%+?

## Side-by-Side Comparison (Most Recent Fiscal Year)

| Metric | ASICS FY2024 (Dec 2024) | Under Armour FY2025 (Mar 2025) |
|---|---|---|
| Revenue | ¥678.5B (~$4.5B) | ~$5.2B |
| Revenue Growth (YoY) | **+18.9%** | -9.4% |
| Gross Margin | **55.8%** | 47.9% |
| SGA % of Revenue | **41.1%** | ~50.2% |
| Operating Margin | **14.7%** | ~-3.6% (GAAP) / ~3.8% (adj.) |
| CapEx % of Revenue | ~3.5% | **3.3%** |

**Key takeaway for Adidas benchmarking:**
- ASICS demonstrates that a performance/specialty footwear brand can achieve 55%+ gross margins and 14%+ operating margins when brand heat, channel mix, and pricing discipline align — a strong aspirational benchmark.
- Under Armour illustrates the operating leverage risk of SGA rigidity in a revenue contraction: a ~$2.6B fixed SGA structure on $5.2B revenue creates structural operating losses.
- Both peers confirm that **gross margin is the primary value driver in footwear** — Adidas' path to margin recovery and peer comparisons should be anchored on gross margin trajectory above all other metrics.
