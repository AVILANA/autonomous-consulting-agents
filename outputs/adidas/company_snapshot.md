**What adidas is:** The world's second-largest sportswear company, with a 75-year brand heritage, ~11% global athletic footwear market share, and a product portfolio spanning performance sport and lifestyle/fashion. [FACT]

**What adidas is not:** A manufacturer; a technology company; a pure-play DTC brand. It is an intellectual property and brand management company that designs in Germany and sources globally. [INFERENCE]

## 4. CHANNEL MIX — WHOLESALE vs. DTC

| Channel | FY2024 Revenue | Share | YoY CN |
|---------|---------------|-------|--------|
| Wholesale | ~€14,200M | ~60% | +14% |
| DTC — Total | ~€9,700M | ~40% | +11% |
| — Own Retail | (within DTC) | — | +15% |
| — E-commerce | ~€4,300M | ~18% of total | +6% |

> DTC/wholesale share figures confirmed directionally from official disclosures; absolute splits at channel level are not explicitly published for FY2024. [FACT — confirmed via annual report segment discussion; exact split INFERENCE]

**FY2025 growth rates:** Wholesale +12% CN; own retail +13% CN; e-commerce +16% CN. [FACT]

**Channel dynamics:**
- Adidas's original "Own the Game" target of DTC = 50% of sales by 2025 was **not achieved** — DTC remains ~40–42%. [FACT]
- CEO Bjørn Gulden deliberately rebalanced toward wholesale, re-engaging strategic retail partners (Foot Locker, Dick's Sporting Goods, JD Sports) that predecessors had de-prioritized. [FACT]
- E-commerce growth of +6% in 2024 was depressed by Yeezy sell-through completing; underlying e-commerce velocity is +16% in 2025 (ex-Yeezy). [FACT/INFERENCE]
- Own stores: **1,933 at end-2024** (2023: 1,863) — 838 concept stores, 1,095 factory outlets. [FACT]

## 5. PRODUCT MIX

| Category | Share of Revenue |
|----------|----------------|
| Footwear | ~57% |
| Apparel | ~35% |
| Accessories / Hardware | ~7% |

**Footwear revenue grew +17% in FY2024** — outperforming apparel — reflecting the global retro sneaker cycle and performance running demand. [FACT]

**Hero franchises:**
- **Lifestyle/Originals:** Samba, Gazelle, Handball Spezial, Superstar, Stan Smith — terrace and retro silhouettes at the center of a global cultural moment [FACT]
- **Performance running:** Adizero (elite), Supernova (volume), Ultraboost (cross-over) [FACT]
- **Football/soccer:** Predator, Copa, X Crazyfast series [FACT]
- **Training/other:** broad product lines across key sports

**Product mix risk:** The Samba/Gazelle/retro cycle that powered gross margin recovery and brand heat since 2023 is inherently trend-dependent. [INFERENCE] Adidas must build durable performance credibility before the lifestyle cycle fades — Gulden has flagged this explicitly. [FACT — earnings call commentary]

## 10. COMPETITIVE POSITION SUMMARY

| Company | Revenue (latest FY) | Operating Margin | Athletic Footwear Share |
|---------|-------------------|-----------------|------------------------|
| **Nike** | ~$46.3B (FY2025, -10%) | ~7–8% (declining) | ~27–30% |
| **Adidas** | €24.8B (FY2025, +5%) | 8.3% (recovering) | ~11% |
| **On Running** | ~CHF 3.0B (+32%) | ~12% | <3% |
| **New Balance** | ~$8B (est., +23%) | N/A private | <5% |
| **PUMA** | ~€8.8B | ~7% | ~5% |

**Net competitive assessment:**
- Nike is in a structural reset — over-indexed DTC, missed running innovation, losing cultural credibility. Adidas is the primary beneficiary in the mass-premium athletic segment. [FACT — Nike FY2025 results confirm; INFERENCE — beneficiary attribution]
- Adidas's gross margin recovery to 50.8% (2024) is structural (better channel/pricing mix, DTC growth, full-price discipline) not cyclical. If sustained, the path to 10%+ EBIT margin by 2028 is credible. [INFERENCE]
- The lifestyle/retro cycle (Samba, Gazelle) will not last indefinitely. The test for adidas is whether performance franchises (running, football) carry the brand when the fashion cycle turns. [INFERENCE]
