# Adidas AG — Peer Set Construction
**Step 3: Peer & Benchmark Analyst | Date: 2026-04-02**

**Sources:** Company filings, press releases, IR publications (FY2024/FY2025 latest available per company)

## 1. PEER SET RATIONALE AND METHODOLOGY

### Selection Criteria
A valid benchmark peer set for adidas must serve three distinct purposes:
1. **Competitive positioning** — who adidas fights for market share and shelf space
2. **Financial comparability** — similar business model or structural position from which adidas can learn
3. **Margin aspiration** — companies that illuminate what adidas's P&L could look like at higher maturity

Peers were selected across three tiers. No single company is a perfect comparable — adidas is the only vertically integrated brand at its scale combining footwear + apparel + lifestyle + performance globally. The peer set is explicitly multi-dimensional.

### What This Peer Set Is NOT
- New Balance is excluded (private company; revenue estimates only; no audited P&L for benchmarking)
- Hanesbrands / VF Corporation / Columbia are excluded (different value chain; mass channel; minimal brand premium)
- Levi Strauss / Gap are excluded (apparel only; no athletic; different competitive dynamics)
- Li-Ning is excluded (primarily domestic China; limited international operations; comparable only on China segment)

### TIER 1: DIRECT FULL-SPECTRUM COMPETITORS
*Companies that compete with adidas across the same broad product/market scope (athletic footwear + apparel, global distribution, branded retail)*

#### PEER 1: NIKE, INC.
| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Ticker | NKE (NYSE) |
| HQ | Beaverton, Oregon, USA |
| FY End | May 31 |
| Latest FY | FY2025 (ending May 2025) |
| Revenue | $46.31B (–9.8% YoY) |
| Operating Margin | ~5.8% (declining) |
| Business model | Same as adidas: outsourced manufacturing, owned brand, global wholesale + DTC |
| Headcount | 77,800 |

**Why included:** Nike is the #1 global athletic brand and adidas's primary strategic reference. Every percentage point Nike loses is available to be captured. Nike's structural reset (2024–2026) is the single biggest market share opportunity in adidas's recent history. Nike is also the aspirational margin benchmark adidas once matched (Nike historically operated at 12–14% EBIT before its current reset). [FACT — Nike filings; INFERENCE — opportunity framing]

**Comparability notes:**
- Nike FY ends May 31; adidas FY ends December 31 — a ~7-month calendar offset must be noted when comparing "latest year"
- Nike reports in USD; adidas in EUR — FX movements create noise in direct revenue comparisons
- Nike's Jordan Brand segment (~15–18% of revenue) has no direct adidas equivalent, inflating Nike's brand premium metrics slightly [INFERENCE]

#### PEER 2: PUMA SE
| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Ticker | PUM (XETRA) |
| HQ | Herzogenaurach, Germany |
| FY End | December 31 |
| Latest FY | FY2024 |
| Revenue | €8.8B (+4.4% currency-adjusted) |
| Operating Margin | 7.1% |
| Business model | Identical to adidas: outsourced manufacturing, licensed brand, wholesale + growing DTC |
| Headcount | 19,599 |

**Why included:** PUMA is adidas's closest structural analog — same business model, same home city, same German listed company (XETRA), same sourcing geography, and same channel structure. PUMA is ~35% of adidas's revenue scale. The comparison isolates the premium/scale effect: why does adidas earn ~1.2pp more EBIT margin despite similar business model? What does PUMA do more efficiently at smaller scale? [FACT — PUMA AR 2024]

**Comparability notes:**
- PUMA's CapEx intensity (~3.0% of revenue) vs. adidas (~2.3%) is unusual for the smaller player — reflects PUMA's higher brick-and-mortar investment per revenue dollar [INFERENCE]
- PUMA's DTC penetration (27.5%) lags adidas (~40%) — structural margin headroom if PUMA closes the gap [FACT]
- PUMA's owner Kering SA (35% stake) provides strategic context but not financial consolidation at brand P&L level [FACT]

#### PEER 3: ON HOLDING AG
| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Ticker | ONON (NYSE) |
| HQ | Zurich, Switzerland |
| FY End | December 31 |
| Latest FY | FY2025 |
| Revenue | CHF 3,014M (+30.0% reported; +35.6% constant currency) |
| Operating Margin | ~12.5% (adjusted EBITDA margin 18.8%) |
| Business model | Premium performance running; outsourced manufacturing; high DTC (41.8%); no meaningful apparel |
| Headcount | ~4,000 |

**Why included:** On is the fastest-growing competitor in adidas's highest-margin segment (premium performance running). On's 62.8% gross margin is the benchmark for what brand premium + category focus can achieve in athletic footwear. On's revenue per employee (~CHF 754K) far exceeds adidas's (~€400K), demonstrating the productivity advantage of a focused, asset-light model. [FACT — On Holding FY2025 results]

**Comparability notes:**
- On competes in a single category (running) and has almost no apparel revenue. Direct comparison must be segment-level, not full P&L [FACT]
- On's net margin (6.8%) is suppressed by a CHF 173M FX loss in FY2025 — adjusted for this, underlying earnings power is materially higher [FACT]
- Roger Federer (investor and ambassador) and the premium lifestyle positioning make On partly a DTC luxury brand, not a pure performance athletic brand — pricing power is structurally different [INFERENCE]
- On has no football, basketball, or lifestyle heritage to monetize — scope is deliberately narrow [FACT]

- Deckers's 42.7% DTC share and DTC comp growth of +13.4% shows what high DTC penetration looks like at operating efficiency
- Hoka's brand awareness is still building globally — it is a US/Europe phenomenon; not yet a comparable in Greater China or emerging markets [FACT]

### TIER 3: MARGIN ASPIRATION BENCHMARKS
*Companies with adjacent positioning (premium apparel, adjacent segment) that demonstrate what adidas's margin structure could look like at higher operating maturity*

---

#### PEER 5: LULULEMON ATHLETICA
| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Ticker | LULU (NASDAQ) |
| HQ | Vancouver, BC, Canada |
| FY End | Late January / early February |
| Latest FY | FY2025 (ending Feb 2025) |
| Revenue | $10.6B |
| Operating Margin | 23.7% |
| Business model | Premium athletic apparel; predominantly DTC/owned retail; minimal wholesale; no footwear scale |
| Headcount | 39,000 |

**Why included:** Lululemon is the clearest illustration of what premium brand positioning + channel control achieves in gross and operating margin in athletic/lifestyle apparel. Lululemon's 59.2% gross margin vs. adidas's ~51% gap is almost entirely attributable to: (1) no wholesale margin give-up, (2) premium pricing without mass-market pressure, (3) highly loyal community model that reduces marketing spend per customer. The 23.7% EBIT margin is the aspirational ceiling for what adidas's margin could reach if it structurally shifted its channel and brand mix. [INFERENCE — analytical framing]

**Comparability notes:**
- Lululemon operates almost exclusively in DTC (own stores + e-commerce); no traditional wholesale. This eliminates the wholesale margin give-up that is ~10–15pp of gross margin for brands selling through department stores and sporting goods chains [INFERENCE — industry knowledge]
- Lululemon has negligible footwear revenue — not a direct competitor in adidas's core category
- Lululemon's FY2025 revenue growth slowed significantly (+4%), suggesting the premium apparel market at this scale has limits — relevant context for adidas's DTC growth aspirations [FACT]

#### PEER 6: ANTA SPORTS PRODUCTS
| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Ticker | 2020.HK (HKEX) |
| HQ | Fujian, China |
| FY End | December 31 |
| Latest FY | FY2024 |
| Revenue | RMB 70.83B (~€9.2B at 2024 avg. FX) (+13.6% YoY) |
| Operating Margin | 26.3% |
| Business model | Multi-brand house (Anta, FILA, Descente, Salomon, Arc'teryx); manufacturing + brand; dominant China; international expansion |
| Headcount | Not publicly disclosed |

**Why included:** Anta is adidas's primary competitive threat in Greater China — adidas's fastest-growing region (~15% of revenue). Anta's 63.4% gross margin and 26.3% operating margin reframe the question: a Chinese branded sportswear company with domestic manufacturing advantages AND premium brand management (owning FILA, Salomon, Arc'teryx) can operate at world-class margins. This is partly explained by home market manufacturing cost advantage and partly by brand portfolio mix. [FACT — Anta FY2024 results; INFERENCE — margin driver analysis]

**Comparability notes:**
- Anta's revenue in RMB reflects ~95% China-derived revenue. International comparability is limited by this geographic concentration
- Anta's gross margin advantage (~12pp vs. adidas) includes a manufacturing cost benefit that adidas, as a 100% outsourced company, does not have
- Anta's acquisition of Salomon, Arc'teryx (via Amer Sports) is expanding its premium addressable market — Anta should be monitored as a global challenger, not just a China domestic competitor [FACT — Anta portfolio disclosed; INFERENCE — strategic direction]
- Amer Sports (Arc'teryx parent) is now separately listed (NYSE: AS); Anta retains a major ownership position [FACT]

#### PEER 7: ASICS CORPORATION
| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Ticker | 7936 (TSE) / ASCCY (OTC ADR) |
| HQ | Kobe, Japan |
| FY End | December 31 |
| Latest FY | FY2024 |
| Revenue | ¥678,526M (~$4.5B / ~€4.1B) (+18.9% YoY) |
| Operating Margin | 14.7% |
| Business model | Performance/specialty footwear (running dominant); outsourced manufacturing; growing DTC; Japan GAAP |
| Headcount | ~9,200 |

**Why included:** ASICS is the most instructive aspirational benchmark in the peer set for adidas's core performance running strategy. It achieves 55.8% gross margin and 14.7% operating margin — metrics that exceed adidas by +4.8pp and +6.4pp respectively — on a smaller but rapidly growing revenue base. ASICS's margin trajectory (49.7% → 52.1% → 55.8% gross margin in three years) demonstrates what channel mix improvement and pricing discipline can achieve in performance footwear. As an Asia-Pacific competitor, ASICS also provides essential geographic diversity to the peer set. [FACT — ASICS FY2024 Consolidated Financial Statements; INFERENCE — aspiration framing]

**Comparability notes:**
- ASICS reports under Japan GAAP; operating profit is directly comparable to EBIT (no interest at operating line). Gross margin and SGA structure are fully comparable to adidas's IFRS P&L at the operational level [FACT]
- ASICS revenue is heavily yen-denominated (~70–75% Japan + Asia) — EUR comparison requires currency adjustment; all EUR figures are approximate using 2024 average FX [INFERENCE]
- ASICS's FY2022–FY2024 revenue CAGR of ~18% is exceptional for a 70+ year-old heritage brand; primarily driven by performance running (including GEL-Nimbus, Kayano, and the running lifestyle "ASICS Tiger" segment) and DTC channel shift [FACT — management commentary in annual reports]
- Discovery question: How much of ASICS's gross margin expansion is attributable to (a) DTC channel shift vs. (b) premium ASP uplift vs. (c) input cost reduction? [ASSUMPTION]

### TIER 4: CAUTIONARY ANALOG
*Company in structural decline and active repositioning in the same category — illustrating the risks of SG&A base rigidity, wholesale over-reliance, and brand identity dilution*

---

#### PEER 8: UNDER ARMOUR, INC.
| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Ticker | UAA / UA (NYSE) |
| HQ | Baltimore, Maryland, USA |
| FY End | March 31 |
| Latest FY | FY2025 (ending March 2025) |
| Revenue | ~$5,163M (–9.4% YoY) |
| Operating Margin | ~–3.6% GAAP; ~3.8% adjusted |
| Business model | Performance athletic apparel and footwear; outsourced; North America–weighted; wholesale-heavy |
| Headcount | ~16,600 |

**Why included:** Under Armour is the most direct cautionary analog in the peer set — a brand at the same scale as adidas was at its 2023 trough, facing structurally similar challenges: over-reliance on wholesale, promotional channel dependency, cost base misaligned to revenue level, and identity dilution from trying to be everything to everyone. Under Armour's "Sell Less, Earn More" repositioning mirrors the discipline adidas imposed under Gulden. The difference is that adidas executed the pivot successfully (EBIT +399% 2023→2024); Under Armour has not yet achieved that inflection. UA is a benchmark for what adidas was trying to avoid — and provides a discovery question framework around what conditions are needed for turnaround success. [FACT — UA FY2025 results; INFERENCE — strategic analog framing]

**Comparability notes:**
- UA's fiscal year ends March 31, creating a ~9-month offset vs. adidas's December 31 year-end — cross-year comparisons need to note this [FACT]
- UA's FY2025 GAAP operating loss (–$185M) includes significant restructuring charges and impairment; adjusted operating income ~$198M (~3.8% margin) is a better representation of ongoing business economics [FACT]
- UA's gross margin trend is improving (44.9% → 46.1% → 47.9%) despite revenue contraction — the "Sell Less, Earn More" strategy is working at the gross line; the problem is SGA is not rightsizing fast enough [FACT/INFERENCE]
- Under Armour has virtually no meaningful Asia-Pacific presence and its Greater China strategy has been inconsistent — limiting comparability on geographic mix [FACT — UA geographic disclosure]

```
          └───────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

```

**Reading the map:**
- adidas is between Nike (larger, lower margin) and On/Lululemon (smaller, much higher margin)
- ASICS now sits above the adidas 2028 target — making it the nearest-term credible aspirational operating margin benchmark in the peer set
- Anta's margin is exceptional given its scale — attributable to vertical integration and home market dominance
- Under Armour below Nike confirms that size alone does not prevent operating leverage failure — the structural lesson for adidas
- The 10%+ EBIT target by 2028 keeps adidas below all premium peers (On, Lululemon, Deckers, ASICS, Anta) but above Nike's current level and well above UA
- On Running's growth trajectory (+30% per year) means it will be ~€7–8B revenue by 2028–2029 at current pace — not a small challenger for long [INFERENCE — extrapolation]
