## RAW PROVOCATION 1 — Demand & Supply Planning

**Title:** "Your demand plan may refresh every 3 weeks. Your €5B inventory depends on knowing what sells today."

**The uncomfortable truth:**
You have o9, SageMaker, and a Databricks Agent Digital Twin running multi-agent AI fleets in production. That is a better planning stack than most consumer goods companies will have by 2030. But the question is not whether the tools exist — it is whether the closed loop runs. When a Samba colorway goes viral on a Tuesday, does your planning engine act in real time or does it wait for the next S&OP cycle? If it waits, the full-price window peaks and closes before your allocation moves. You either stock out on what is hot or hold too much of what is not.

ASICS added 610 basis points (pp) of gross margin in 3 years — from 49.7% to 55.8% — not by launching new products but by knowing exactly what to stock at full price and what to cut. That is a planning system that works. adidas has a 4.8pp gross margin gap vs. ASICS and carries €4,989M in inventory on €23.7B of revenue — a 21% ratio that sits above the peer median. The gap between what your planning stack theoretically enables and what the closed loop actually delivers could be worth 0.5pp of gross margin, or ~€124M on today's revenue base.

**Key evidence:**
- Inventory €4,989M (FY2024) on €23,683M revenue = 21% inventory-to-revenue — above peer median [FACT]
- o9 Solutions, SageMaker, Databricks Agent Digital Twin all confirmed in production [FACT]

## RAW PROVOCATION 3 — Ship-from-Store / Fulfillment Speed

**Title:** "838 concept stores ready to ship in hours. Customers wait 3–7 days. You pay Amazon to fix it."

**The uncomfortable truth:**
You have 838 concept stores in major cities. RFID inventory accuracy above 99%. GK OmniPOS with ship-from-store capability at 1,300 locations. The physical network for 1-day delivery in every major US and European city is already built and paid for. You own it.

But when someone orders on adidas.com today, the order goes to Wilkes-Barre, Pennsylvania, and arrives in 3–7 days. Amazon Prime delivers the same shoe in 1–2 days. Your response to this gap was to sign with Amazon Multi-Channel Fulfillment (MCF) in February 2025. That is a pragmatic call. But you are paying Amazon to solve a speed problem using your own inventory, when your own store might be 15 minutes from the customer.

The routing engine — the one that connects an incoming adidas.com order to the nearest concept store with stock, checks live RFID inventory, selects the right carrier, and prints the label at the store — is not confirmed to exist at scale. This is not a Body problem. The stores are there. The RFID is there. The POS systems are there. This is a 6–12 month Brain build. And with the FIFA World Cup 2026 running in the US, Canada, and Mexico from June to July, the North America fulfillment window matters more this year than any recent cycle. ASICS runs ~25–30% Direct-to-Consumer (DTC) and earned 14.7% operating margin. adidas runs ~40% DTC and earns 8.3%. Part of that gap is the cost of being slow.

**Key evidence:**
- 838 concept stores (urban) with RFID >99% accuracy; GK OmniPOS ship-from-store confirmed at 1,300+ locations [FACT]
- US DTC standard delivery: 3–7 business days; Amazon Prime: 1–2 days [FACT]
