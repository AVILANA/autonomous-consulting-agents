## 2. MACRO AND SECTOR DYNAMICS — 2025–2026

### 2.1 Health and Wellness Tailwind

**Running boom:** Global participation in running and trail running at all-time highs. Half marathon and marathon registrations growing. This is structural — driven by Gen Z/Millennial identity around health — not a post-COVID blip. [FACT — race registration data; industry commentary]

**Implication for adidas:** Performance running credibility is increasingly a brand prerequisite. On Running and Hoka captured the "serious runner" segment that Nike and adidas historically owned. Adidas's Adizero and Supernova lines are competing but have not yet recaptured the mindshare they held a decade ago. [FACT — On/Hoka market share data; INFERENCE — adidas relative position]

### 2.2 DTC / Digital Saturation

- DTC was the growth vector of 2017–2022 for every major sportswear brand. By 2025, **DTC capability is table stakes, not a differentiator.** [INFERENCE — market maturity observation]
- The brands winning on DTC are winning on **loyalty ecosystem depth** — not just online selling capability. Nike SNKRS, adidas Confirmed/Creators Club, PUMA's membership program all target the same behavior: creating a repeat, high-engagement buyer who buys at full price through the brand's own channels.
- **Nike over-rotated** on DTC (2022–2024): cut ~50,000 wholesale doors, then lost revenue, brand heat, and market share when DTC alone could not fill the gap. Now rebuilding wholesale relationships it spent years damaging. [FACT — Nike FY2025 commentary]
- **Adidas re-balanced** under Gulden: deliberately rebuilt wholesale while growing DTC selectively. This is now the correct playbook — wholesale + DTC as complementary, not competing. [FACT — management commentary; INFERENCE — strategic validation]

### 2.3 China: Recovery With Ongoing Structural Risk

**Recovery:** Greater China is adidas's fastest-growing major region (+10% CN in 2024, +13% in 2025). Post-lockdown consumption recovery and sustained brand desirability among Chinese Gen Z are the drivers. [FACT]

**Structural risk:**
- **Domestic Chinese brands** (Anta, Li-Ning, PEAK, Erke) have grown rapidly, are well-funded, and are competing effectively in the mid-market and sports performance segments within China. [FACT — Anta and Li-Ning financial disclosures]
- Anta Group revenues exceeded €12B in 2025, competing directly with adidas in Chinese consumers' consideration. [FACT — estimated from Anta press releases]
- **Geopolitical risk is real but currently subdued.** Past boycott incidents (e.g., 2021 Xinjiang cotton controversy) demonstrate the vulnerability of Western brands to nationalist consumer sentiment in China. Adidas navigated the 2021 episode without lasting damage but the risk profile remains. [FACT — 2021 boycott; INFERENCE — ongoing exposure]
- **Nike is losing China.** Nike's Greater China revenue declined significantly in FY2025, driven by losing relevance vs. domestic brands and geopolitical sensitivity. [FACT — Nike FY2025 results] Adidas is benefiting from Nike's China weakness in the short term. [INFERENCE]

### 2.4 US Tariffs and Trade Disruption

- **Section 301 tariffs on Chinese goods** and retaliatory tariff structures have created a structural shift in global athletic footwear sourcing. Goods sourced in China for sale in the US face tariff rates that make the business model uneconomical. [FACT]
- **Industry-wide response:** Every major sportswear company is reducing/eliminating China-origin US-bound production. Vietnam, Indonesia, Cambodia, India are the primary beneficiaries. [FACT — supply chain reporting across Nike, adidas, PUMA]
- **Adidas specifically:** Stopped procuring US-bound goods from China; ~€200M embedded tariff headwind in 2026 guidance. [FACT]
- **Risk escalation scenario:** If tariffs expand to Vietnam or other Southeast Asian production centers, the supply chain disruption would be material across the entire industry. No incumbent has a meaningful US domestic manufacturing base. [INFERENCE — scenario analysis]

### 2.5 Sustainability as Brand Differentiator → Cost Driver

- **Consumer expectation:** >54% of Gen Z cite eco-conscious materials as a top-3 purchase factor. [FACT — Deloitte/McKinsey survey data as reported in industry publications]
- **Reality:** Sustainable materials typically cost more. The economics of sustainability only work at scale with supply chain investment.
- **adidas:** 90% of articles using preferred/sustainable materials targeted by 2025. [FACT] This is a genuine strategic commitment, not just narrative — it is the only ESG metric tied to executive compensation. [FACT]
- **Regulatory pressure:** EU Ecodesign for Sustainable Products Regulation (ESPR) and corporate sustainability reporting (CSRD — in force for large EU companies from 2024) are raising the bar for EU-listed companies specifically. Adidas, as a German SE, is in the first wave of CSRD reporting requirements. [FACT]

### 3.2 The Nike Structural Reset — A Generational Window for Adidas

**Nike's predicament (2024–2026):**
- FY2025 (ending May 2025) revenue: ~$46.3B, -10% — the worst annual decline in Nike's modern history. [FACT]
- Net income FY2025: ~$3.2B, -45% YoY. [FACT]
- Nike stock: trading near 9-year lows as of April 2026. [FACT]
- Root causes: (1) over-invested in DTC, cut wholesale; (2) missed the running boom — Hoka and On took the serious runner while Nike focused on Dunk/Jordan lifestyle; (3) innovation pipeline slowed; (4) Greater China revenue declining; (5) management instability (CEO Elliott Hill returned from retirement Oct 2024 to replace John Donahoe). [FACT — multiple financial press sources]

**Implication for adidas:**
- Every percentage point of market share Nike loses is contested primarily between adidas, On, New Balance, and Hoka.
- Adidas is the only brand with the scale, retail presence, and product breadth to absorb Nike's wholesale exit from thousands of doors. JD Sports, Foot Locker, and sports retailers actively preferencing adidas in premium positioning. [INFERENCE — plausible from reported wholesale partnership announcements]
- **The window is real but not permanent.** Nike is restructuring with urgency. By 2027–2028, Nike's product pipeline and channel health could recover. The time to cement share gains is 2025–2026. [INFERENCE]

### 3.3 On Running — The Premium Performance Threat

**On's trajectory:**
- Revenue +32% in FY2025. Revenue CAGR since IPO (2021): ~50%. [FACT — On financial disclosures]
- Premium pricing: On's average selling price is ~30–40% above adidas running equivalents. [INFERENCE from retail pricing data]
- Target consumer: premium, health-conscious, affluent runner — overlaps with adidas's DTC high-value customer. [INFERENCE]
- **On is not competing in football, lifestyle heritage, or emerging markets** — its scope is narrow (premium performance, primarily running). adidas should not overestimate On as a comprehensive threat. [INFERENCE]

### 3.4 Hoka — The Running Boom Beneficiary

- Hoka has taken the maximal cushioning, "super shoe" segment and built a health/wellness lifestyle identity around it. [FACT]
- Hoka's growth rate (+28% est. FY2025) is driven by both serious runners and casual wellness adopters attracted by the aesthetic and comfort positioning. [FACT — Deckers/Hoka financial disclosures]
- **For adidas:** The Adizero line is a direct competitor in race/elite running. Supernova competes in the volume everyday running segment. Hoka pressure is real in the US and growing in Europe. [INFERENCE]

**Where adidas captures value:**

- **The brand IS the business.** adidas's sustainable competitive advantage is not supply chain, not retail, not technology — it is the brand's cultural and performance credibility. Everything else is infrastructure. [INFERENCE — analytical framing]

## 8. SECTOR CONTEXT SUMMARY — WHAT THIS MEANS FOR A PURSUIT

**The macro setup is favorable for adidas:**
- Global sportswear grows structurally; adidas has secular tailwinds in its core category
- Nike's stumble is a multi-year window; adidas is the best-positioned beneficiary
- China recovery is real and adidas is capturing it faster than peers

**The structural risks are real:**
- US tariffs are a known headwind; any Southeast Asia escalation is systemic risk to the whole industry
- Domestic Chinese brands are competitive and gaining resources; China is not a "safe" growth market
- The retro cycle (Samba, Gazelle) is trend-dependent; product pipeline diversification is a strategic necessity

**The margin opportunity is significant:**
- adidas at 8.3% EBIT margin is trading below its own historical level (9.4% in 2021 before crises) and well below premium peers (On at 12%, Lululemon at 23%)
- The path to 10%+ EBIT by 2028 requires ~1.7pp of margin expansion over 3 years — achievable through revenue leverage on SG&A, but tight if revenue growth slows or macros deteriorate
- Every basis point of gross margin improvement (from channel mix, pricing, DTC growth) is worth ~€25M at current revenue scale

**For an AI/transformation engagement:**
- The CFO owns technology and supply chain — all technology investment proposals should be framed around margin recovery and capital efficiency, not innovation theater
- TRANS4RM (SAP S/4HANA) is the critical dependency — no AI/analytics initiative can reach full potential until the data backbone is unified
- Supply chain resilience (tariff response, demand sensing, inventory optimization) is the most operationally urgent area for technology investment
- The 2028 margin target creates urgency: the window for SG&A-impacting transformation is 2025–2027
