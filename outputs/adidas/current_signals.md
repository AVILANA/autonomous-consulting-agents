## 1. LEADERSHIP AND GOVERNANCE CHANGES

### 1.1 Bjørn Gulden Contract Extended to December 31, 2030
**Date:** March 4, 2026 | **Source:** adidas-group.com [FACT]

The Supervisory Board extended Gulden's CEO mandate through end-2030, announced simultaneously with FY2025 results. The extension was pre-emptive — not in response to pressure — and was paired with the share buyback announcement as a coordinated signal of board confidence.

**Strategic implication:** Succession uncertainty is removed for five years. The brand-first, wholesale-inclusive, product-led strategy is locked in. Any technology or transformation engagement must be positioned as accelerating Gulden's declared priorities (margin recovery to >10%, North America, DTC data activation) — not as a strategic redirection. [INFERENCE]

### 1.2 Supervisory Board Chair Transition: Nassef Sawiris Replacing Thomas Rabe
**Date:** March 4, 2026 | **Source:** adidas-group.com, US News [FACT]

Thomas Rabe ends his chairmanship at the close of the May 7, 2026 AGM in Fürth. The board proposes Nassef Sawiris — Deputy Chairman and holder of ~7% of adidas shares via NNS Group — as incoming Chairman, pending shareholder approval. New board member proposed: Mathias Dopfner (CEO, Axel Springer SE).

**Strategic implication (Sawiris):** Sawiris is simultaneously adidas's largest individual shareholder and its prospective board chair. This is ownership-aligned governance — capital allocation decisions (buyback, M&A, investment in tech) will be faster and less bureaucratic. An engaged owner-chairman typically shortens time-to-decision on strategic bets. [INFERENCE]

**Strategic implication (Dopfner):** Axel Springer is a media and digital publishing conglomerate. Dopfner's presence on the board signals heightened interest in digital content strategy, media partnerships, and perhaps brand-as-media evolution. Unusual profile for a sportswear board. [INFERENCE]

### 1.3 No CTO or CDO at Executive Board Level — Gap Confirmed
**Date:** Ongoing | **Source:** adidas 2025 Annual Report [FACT]

Technology and Supply Chain remain under CFO Harm Ohlmeyer (a structural consolidation made August 2024). There is no Chief Technology Officer, Chief Digital Officer, or Chief AI Officer at Executive Board level.

**Strategic implication:** AI and technology investment decisions are filtered through a P&L and efficiency lens, not a product or growth lens. This limits the mandate for transformative AI bets and creates a sponsorship gap. For any engagement, the CFO is the primary economic buyer; the CEO must be the vision sponsor. [INFERENCE]

## 3. TECHNOLOGY AND AI SIGNALS — MORE ADVANCED THAN PUBLICLY COMMUNICATED

This is the most important signal cluster for pursuit positioning. Adidas's actual AI maturity is substantially ahead of its investor communications, which rarely reference technology.

### 3.1 Agentic AI — Moving from Deployment to Governance [FACT]

At the **Databricks Data + AI Summit 2026** (June, San Francisco), adidas is presenting a session titled:

> **"Beyond the Trace: adidas' Agent Digital Twin for Governance, Cost, and ROI"**

The session describes a lakehouse control plane that maps agent → tool → prompt → retrieval → model call → post-processing and rolls up risk, quality, and unit economics across an **agent fleet** — implying multiple AI agents running in production simultaneously. This is not a pilot; it is a governance architecture for production multi-agent systems.

**Progression:** At the Databricks Data + AI Summit 2025 (prior year), adidas presented on "The Next Wave of AI Applications Driven by Agentic Workflow at adidas" — showing production results:
- 2M+ product reviews processed annually
- 60% latency reduction (15.5s → 6s)
- 91.67% cost savings via LLM optimization
- 98.5% token efficiency (200K tokens → 3K)
- Insights delivered to 500+ internal decision-makers across 150+ countries

**Strategic implication:** The 12-month jump from "building agentic workflows" to "governing agent digital twins" is a significant maturation signal. Adidas is operating at a level of AI production sophistication that is genuinely ahead of most consumer goods companies. The internal capability is real — but it sits in pockets (Databricks, AWS Bedrock) without an integrated enterprise AI strategy or a C-suite champion. [INFERENCE]

### 3.2 GitHub Copilot — 85% of Engineers Using Daily [FACT]

Adidas piloted GitHub Copilot with 500 engineers (Q1 2024). By six months post-pilot, ~700 developers (85% of the engineering organization) were using it daily. Reported outcomes: 20–25% productivity uplift; 91% of pilot engineers said they did not want to work without it.

**Strategic implication:** Developer AI adoption is genuine and high — this is not a showcase metric. The engineering culture is AI-forward at the practitioner level. This makes adidas a credible AI partner, not a laggard to be educated. Position engagement accordingly. [INFERENCE]

### 3.3 AI Archive — Generative Design on 75 Years of Footwear History [FACT]

Adidas developed **AI Archive** — a diffusion model trained on the full archive of adidas footwear designs since the 1950s — deployed to designers as a web application for generative design inspiration. This is a consumer-product AI application that directly accelerates product creation.

### 3.4 GenAI for Business Intelligence — AWS Bedrock + Databricks [FACT]

Adidas is using Amazon Bedrock as an internal knowledge-base layer for engineering teams (VP of Enterprise Architecture described it as "a highly valued addition to their generative AI toolkit"). Separately, adidas and Traeger Grills co-presented on Amazon Bedrock's natural language data visualization — enabling non-technical users to create charts and insights without code.

### 3.5 TRANS4RM (SAP S/4HANA on AWS) — Completion Status Unclear [FACT/INFERENCE]

Central Finance went live November 2023. Finance and Purchasing were planned for 2024. As of April 2, 2026, **no public announcement of full TRANS4RM completion exists** — not in the 2025 Annual Report, not in any press release, not in the earnings call. IT capex remains a line in FY2025 capital expenditure (part of €540M total capex). [FACT — absence of completion announcement; INFERENCE — still in progress]

**Strategic implication:** Until TRANS4RM completes, adidas's full AI potential is gated. The Sales module and regional deployments are likely still in progress. This creates an opening to help adidas design the AI intelligence layer that will sit on top of the completed ERP — before TRANS4RM is done, so the architecture is ready at go-live. [INFERENCE]

## 4. SUPPLY CHAIN AND TARIFF SIGNALS

### 4.1 project44 Partnership — Production AI Under World Cup Pressure [FACT]
**Date:** February 15, 2026 | **Source:** Retail Technology Innovation Hub

Adidas signed with **project44** — an AI-powered supply chain visibility and decision intelligence platform — after project44 CEO Jett McCandless described pursuing adidas for "eight years." The deal context: (a) World Cup supply chain execution (hard deadline, massive volume); (b) long-term AI-driven supply chain intelligence strategy.

**Strategic implication:** This is not a pilot — it is a production commitment with operational stakes. The World Cup imposes a hard forcing function: adidas must deliver kit to 13 national teams and official match balls (including the AI-chipped Trionda) under a fixed calendar. project44 is the real-time visibility layer. [INFERENCE]

### 4.2 China Sourcing Exit — US Market [FACT]
CEO Gulden confirmed adidas has "basically tried not to source any products anymore from China into the U.S." US-bound goods now come from Vietnam (27% of total production), Indonesia (19%), Cambodia, and other SE Asian countries. China-made goods are sold to Chinese consumers only.

**Strategic implication:** The supply chain restructuring is complete for the China/US vector. The residual tariff risk is Vietnam and Indonesia exposure — both were targeted in prior US trade actions. No sourcing configuration is fully tariff-proof. [INFERENCE]

### 4.3 Tariff Financial Impact and Response Posture [FACT]
- FY2026 operating profit headwind from tariffs: ~€200M
- Gross margin impact in 2025: ~50bp (without tariffs, gross margin would have exceeded 52%)
- Price increases: selective (Samba raised $90 → $100 on premium SKUs); budget pricing defended
- Pre-tariff shipment front-loading executed ahead of April 4–9, 2026 effective dates
- CEO: "In a normal world" guidance would have been higher; tariff uncertainty prevents it

**US tariff policy context (as of April 2, 2026):**
- Feb 20, 2026: Supreme Court ruled prior sweeping tariffs illegal
- Feb 22, 2026: Trump signed new 10% global tariff under Section 122 of Trade Act of 1974
- 15% replacement level floated
- Section 122 tariff expires July 24, 2026 — creating a second guidance inflection point in H2 2026
- FDRA survey: 25%+ of footwear brands expect landed costs to rise 11–20%

## 7. COMPETITOR SIGNALS

### 7.1 Nike — Turnaround Early Positive Signs, Tariff Exposure Severe [FACT]

- CEO Elliott Hill's "Win Now" strategy showing early traction: Q1 FY2026 (Aug 2025) revenue +1% — first positive quarter after multiple declines; wholesale +7%, North America +4%
- Tariff exposure: Nike expects $1.5B tariff hit and 1.2pp gross margin compression in FY2026
- Nike gross margin: 42.2% vs. adidas ~51.6% — the structural gap has widened over the past two years
- Nike is clearing stale DTC-era inventory and reinvesting in wholesale relationships — the exact playbook adidas executed successfully under Gulden

**Adidas implication:** The two-year window of Nike weakness (2024–2025) was a direct tailwind for adidas's market share gains and brand recovery. If Nike's turnaround accelerates through 2026–2027, adidas faces renewed direct competition in running, basketball, and North America. The structural gross margin gap (+9pp in adidas's favor) is a durable advantage if maintained. [INFERENCE]

### 7.2 New Balance — $9.2B (+19%) — Most Direct Lifestyle Threat [FACT]

- FY2025 revenue: $9.2B; fifth consecutive year of double-digit growth
- Target: $10B by end of 2026
- 80 new retail stores opened globally in 2025
- Actively targeting Nike's retro sneaker consumer with the "Gator Run" launch; competing directly in the 574/990 vs. Samba/Gazelle segment
- Private company — no tariff-specific disclosure; similar Vietnam/Indonesia sourcing exposure

**Adidas implication:** New Balance is the most direct competitive threat to adidas in the lifestyle/heritage sneaker category — the segment that has driven adidas's margin recovery. Both brands are competing for the same consumer (aged 25–40, culture-aware, willing to pay premium for heritage authenticity). New Balance's growth velocity is faster than adidas's in this segment. ### 7.3 PUMA — Completing Reset; Anta/Kering Capital Injection [FACT]

## 9. SYNTHESIS — PURSUIT TIMING AND URGENCY

### Why pursue adidas now vs. in 12 months?

**1. The AI governance gap is immediate and undisclosed.**
Adidas is operating production-scale agentic AI (Databricks Agent Digital Twin 2026 session) without an enterprise AI strategy, a board-level AI mandate, or a CTO/CDO. The architecture is fragmented: Databricks for analytics, AWS Bedrock for GenAI, project44 for supply chain, SageMaker for ML, o9 for planning. Each is a point solution. No one is connecting them. This is the most actionable near-term engagement entry point. [INFERENCE from FACT on tech stack and governance gap]

**2. TRANS4RM is approaching the post-go-live integration window.**
With TRANS4RM likely completing Sales module deployment in 2026–2027, the pre-go-live period is the optimal time to design the AI/analytics architecture that will sit on top. Waiting until after go-live means retrofitting; arriving now means co-designing. [INFERENCE]

**3. The World Cup is a forcing function — with a North America data gap.**
The June–July 2026 World Cup will generate the largest brand moment adidas has experienced in its weakest market. If adidas's DTC and personalization infrastructure is not ready to capture and activate new consumers during the World Cup, the brand window will close. There is approximately 10–12 weeks to help adidas prepare before the tournament begins. [INFERENCE]

**4. North America underperformance is an acknowledged problem without a public solution.**
The CFO/CEO acknowledge North America is behind; the street is watching for evidence of recovery; and the World Cup is the planned catalyst. But there is no publicly announced North America digital acceleration program. This is a white space. [INFERENCE from FACT on guidance and management commentary]

**5. New governance (Sawiris as Chair) creates faster decision-making.**
Incoming owner-chairman with 7% stake and business operator background will accelerate large strategic investments that a more traditional Supervisory Board might slow. The first six months of a new chairman's tenure is typically the highest-velocity decision window. [INFERENCE]
