### 4.2 Strategic Priorities — Reconstructed from Earnings Calls and Annual Report MD&A

The following priorities are attributable to Gulden's public statements but have **not been presented as a formal strategy document**:

**1. "Glocalization" — local relevance at global scale**
Gulden has repeatedly stated that adidas needs to be relevant in local sports and cultures, not just global mega-events. Examples: cricket in South Asia, local football leagues, emerging market brand-building. [FACT — earnings call attribution]

**2. Full-price discipline and channel health**
A consistent theme: adidas must sell at full price through premium channels. The Yeezy episode and 2022 margin collapse reinforced that discounting destroys brand value. Gulden explicitly targets improving the sell-through rate and reducing off-price dependency. [FACT]

**3. Wholesale re-engagement as brand amplification**
Reversal of the prior DTC-at-any-cost posture. Foot Locker, JD Sports, Dick's Sporting Goods, and similar strategic partners are viewed as brand-building amplifiers, not channel conflicts. [FACT — earnings call commentary]

**4. Product innovation — performance credentials**
Adidas must be credible in sport, not merely fashionable. Gulden has flagged that the Samba/Gazelle retro cycle will fade and the company must build durable performance franchises in running and football. [FACT — earnings call commentary]

**5. Supply chain localization ("China makes for China")**
US tariffs and geopolitical dynamics are pushing adidas toward a demand-region-matched supply model. China-to-US sourcing has been explicitly reduced. Vietnam, Indonesia, and Cambodia absorb US-bound volume. India is a nascent manufacturing hub. [FACT]

**6. Digital foundation — TRANS4RM completion**
The SAP S/4HANA migration must complete. Everything else (AI, ML, demand sensing, supply chain intelligence) depends on the data infrastructure becoming fully operational. [INFERENCE — logical dependency from reported tech roadmap; TRANS4RM status as multi-year ongoing is FACT]

**7. Margin recovery to >10% by 2028**
This is the single most financially specific commitment. The path runs through: gross margin stability at ~51–52% + SG&A leverage as revenue scales + operational efficiency. [FACT — target; INFERENCE — mechanism]

## 6. KEY TRANSFORMATION PROGRAMS IN FLIGHT

### 6.1 TRANS4RM — ERP Modernization (Most Critical)

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Program name | TRANS4RM |
| Scope | Full ERP migration to SAP S/4HANA on AWS |
| Scale | Described as one of the largest S/4HANA installations globally [FACT] |
| Status | SAP Central Finance live: November 2023 [FACT]; Finance + Purchasing planned live 2024 [FACT]; full rollout ongoing |
| Investment | Part of >€1B digital investment commitment under "Own the Game" [FACT] |
| Platform partner | AWS (primary cloud); includes Amazon SageMaker for ML workloads [FACT] |
| AI/ML layer | o9 Solutions for retail planning and DTC optimization [FACT]; project44 for supply chain intelligence (adopted Feb 2026) [FACT] |
| GenAI | Early-stage experimentation; engineering team building internal GenAI tooling [FACT — The New Stack reporting] |

**Key risk:** TRANS4RM is a multi-year transformation with no publicly confirmed completion date. Until the full S/4HANA backbone is operational, data integration across functions (demand sensing, inventory optimization, supply chain execution) remains limited by legacy system fragmentation. [INFERENCE — characteristic of large ERP transformations]

### 6.2 Digital Product Creation

3D design tools deployed across product creation process as part of "Own the Game" digital backbone. [FACT] Reduces physical sample requirements; accelerates time-to-market. Status of full deployment not publicly quantified.

### 6.3 Retail Network Optimization

New flagship openings (Champs-Elysées, Paris — opened for Paris 2024 Olympics). [FACT] Focus on quality and elevation of existing fleet, not aggressive net new door counts. CapEx ~50% directed to controlled space. [FACT]

### 6.4 Headcount Restructuring (HQ)

Up to 500 HQ jobs cut in Herzogenaurach (announced January 2025). [FACT] Rationale: reduce complexity, decentralize decision-making to local markets. Restructuring charges impacted 2025 guidance.
