## CONDITION FLAGS — EVALUATION

### Flag 1: Financial Distress?
**Answer: NO**

Evidence:
- EBIT recovered from €268M (1.3% margin, FY2023) to ~€2,059M (8.3% margin, FY2025) — +669% in 2 years [FACT]
- Free cash flow ~€2.37B (FY2024); €1B share buyback launched; dividend +40% [FACT]
- Net hiring mode in Q1 2026; no announced layoffs [FACT]
- Balance sheet: Debt/Equity ~0.95x (FY2024) — moderate and declining [FACT]

**Routing decision:** Do NOT weight provocations toward cost reduction as the primary theme. Weight toward margin expansion, operational precision, and competitive velocity. A company in recovery mode with a 10%+ EBIT target by 2028 needs growth and efficiency — not crisis surgery.

---

### Flag 2: High Growth — Weight Scalability?
**Answer: MODERATE GROWTH — not scalability-first**

Evidence:
- Revenue CN growth +13% (FY2025) — second-highest in peer set after On Holding [FACT]
- Reported growth only +4.8% due to EUR appreciation [FACT]
- North America is the structural laggard at +4% CN vs. +13% global [FACT]
- Management guidance for FY2026: high-single-digit CN growth — measured, not explosive [FACT]

**Routing decision:** Growth is healthy but not at the "hyper-scale" level that requires scalability-first architecture. Provocations should address operational precision at current scale, not infrastructure scaling. Exception: FIFA World Cup 2026 creates a near-term demand surge window in North America that warrants a fulfillment scalability lens for that specific geography.

---

### Flag 3: Standout Peer Found?
**Answer: YES — ASICS CORPORATION is the confirmed standout peer**

Evidence:
- ASICS operating margin: 7.0% (FY2022) → 9.5% (FY2023) → **14.7% (FY2024)** [FACT — ASICS annual reports]
- Margin improvement: +770 basis points in 2 years — nearly doubled [FACT]
- Mechanism: gross margin expansion of +610 bps (49.7% → 55.8%) via channel discipline + ASP improvement [FACT]
- SG&A held stable (~41%) while gross margin expanded — operating leverage flowed through gross profit line [INFERENCE — HIGH]
- Revenue CAGR: +18% per year — demonstrating that margin and growth co-exist [FACT]
- ASICS is directly comparable to adidas: outsourced manufacturing, athletic footwear dominant, performance running focus [FACT]
- Closest attainable aspirational benchmark in the peer set — more credible than Lululemon (23.7%) or Anta (26.3%) given structural comparability [INFERENCE — HIGH]

**Why ASICS is the standout peer (not On, Lululemon, or Anta):**
On (12.5%) and Lululemon (23.7%) operate fundamentally different business models — no wholesale, no multi-category complexity, DTC-dominant. Their margins are not directly replicable for adidas. Anta (26.3%) benefits from vertical integration and home market dominance. ASICS is the only peer that:
(a) is structurally analogous (outsourced, multi-market, wholesale + DTC mix),
(b) achieved its improvement in 2 years not 5, and
(c) did it through the same lever adidas is pulling — full-price discipline and DTC channel improvement.

**Routing decision:** ASICS must appear as central evidence in at least 2 of the 5 provocations. The message to the adidas COO: "ASICS did in 2 years what you need to do by 2028. Here is what they did differently."

### Flag 4: Body Primary Constraint in 2+ Areas?
**Answer: NO — This is a Brain-First opportunity**

Evidence from body_brain_diagnosis.md:
- Overall Brain Maturity: 3.3/5 — intermediate and unevenly deployed [FACT — diagnosis]
- Overall Body Adequacy: 3.5/5 — largely fixed and functional [FACT — diagnosis]
- TRANS4RM is the single most important infrastructure program — Brain completion, not Body rebuild [FACT]
- The highest-impact velocity unlock (ship-from-store) requires ZERO Body investment — it is a Brain build on existing stores [FACT/INFERENCE — HIGH]
- Velocity bottleneck classification: SC-1 (Procurement), SC-2 (Demand Planning), SC-4 (Inbound), SC-6 (Control Tower), SC-7 (Freight Audit), SC-8 (Returns) are all classified BRAIN [FACT — body_brain_diagnosis velocity table]
- Only SC-3 and SC-5 have Body + Brain elements — and even these are dominated by Brain gaps (routing engine, carrier APIs) not missing physical assets

**Body strengths that should NOT be framed as gaps:**
- Mantova: €350M invested, 700+ robots, 500K shipments/day peak — world-class [FACT]
- Wilkes-Barre: 843K sq ft, 2-hour order-to-pack, 200K units/day — world-class [FACT]
- Stores: 1,933 with RFID >99% accuracy — excellent physical foundation [FACT]

**Routing decision:** Do NOT lead with network optimization. Do NOT propose new DC investment. The consulting scope is Brain activation: connecting the intelligence layer to the existing Body assets adidas has already built. Provocations should frame the gap as "you have the physical network — the intelligence layer is what is missing."
