# Maersk Management Roadmap

## Priority themes
- [FACT] Strengthen and modernize global supply chains to support customer partnerships.
- [FACT] Leverage Maersk’s unique asset footprint as a competitive advantage.
- [FACT] Deliver operational excellence and cost discipline.
- [INFERENCE] Execute fleet renewal and asset utilization improvements in ocean and terminals.
- [INFERENCE] Advance decarbonization and ESG as central strategic themes.

## Transformation themes underway
- [INFERENCE] Maersk is balancing asset-heavy network modernization with customer-facing logistics integration.
- [INFERENCE] The annual report highlights progress across Ocean, Terminals, and Logistics & Services as a cohesive transformation story.

## Digital / AI / automation priorities
- [INFERENCE] The public language focuses on modernization of supply chains rather than explicit AI claims.
- [ASSUMPTION] Digital and automation investments are likely being positioned as enablers of reliability, asset utilization, and customer visibility.

## Customer, service, cost, and productivity signals
- [FACT] Management uses customer partnership and the asset footprint as the foundation for performance.
- [FACT] Cost discipline is explicitly linked to delivering value and maintaining competitiveness.
- [INFERENCE] Productivity and service reliability are implicitly prioritized through the focus on operational excellence.

## Areas management avoids or under-discusses
- [INFERENCE] There is limited public explicit discussion of specific digital/AI performance metrics or time-bound transformation milestones.
- [INFERENCE] The disclosure does not surface detailed risks around integration complexity between ocean, terminals, and logistics.
- [ASSUMPTION] There is little public detail on how Maersk measures progress on end-to-end digital orchestration or automation ROI.

## Where our offering reinforces their agenda
- Helping Maersk translate supply-chain modernization into real-time orchestration and decision support.
- Delivering asset utilization improvements that reinforce cost discipline and reliability targets.
- Supporting carbon and sustainability reporting through data-driven operational planning.

## Where we can constructively challenge their approach
- Push beyond asset-heavy reliability to deliver faster, algorithmic decision-making across the full logistics stack.
- Challenge any narrow focus on operations execution without equal emphasis on cross-functional digital integration and data governance.
- Question whether current transformation plans sufficiently address the cost of network complexity and manual handoffs.

## Recommended pitch language
- “We can help Maersk make its differentiated asset footprint a digitally orchestrated, AI-enabled source of reliability and cost discipline.”
- “The most concrete value is not just in modernizing supply chains, but in giving operations the data and decision support needed to translate that ambition into consistent execution.”
