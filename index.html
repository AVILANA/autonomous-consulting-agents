Section 1 — The Provocations

Four supply chain hypotheses, each with a quantified cost of inaction

Demand Planning

Your demand plan is 3 weeks behind the market. Your €4.99B inventory proves it.

Working Capital Release Planning Cycle Speed

Primary buyers: CFO · CSCO · COO

  • › adidas closed FY2024 with €4.99B in inventory — 20% of annual revenue — despite record brand heat and full-price sell-through on lifestyle products like the Samba and Gazelle. The inventory level reflects a planning system calibrated for a world that moves slower than today's consumer. [FACT — FY2024 Annual Report]
  • › Demand sensing from a social trend to a planning update is estimated at 2–4 weeks. Best-in-class brands using real-time consumer signals update allocation within 48–72 hours. The gap is not technology — adidas runs o9 Solutions for in-season planning and Amazon SageMaker for seasonal forecasting. The gap is in the input: both systems operate on historical sell-in orders from retailers, not real-time sell-through from consumers. [INFERENCE — from velocity benchmarks; FACT on platform deployments]
  • › The Samba and Gazelle lifestyle cycle that powered the recovery is explicitly flagged by CEO Gulden as having a natural end. When the next cycle turns, adidas's planning system will detect it the same way it detected the last one — through retailer reorders, weeks after the consumer has already moved on. [INFERENCE — Gulden earnings call commentary]

Cost of Inaction

€248M / year

Every 1 percentage point of gross margin lost to forecast-driven markdowns and stockouts costs €248M in operating profit at current revenue scale. A 5% improvement in forecast accuracy reduces inventory carrying cost by ~€250M in working capital. Neither happens while the demand signal refreshes monthly. [FACT on FY2025 revenue €24.8B; FACT on inventory base; INFERENCE on gross margin leverage at 51.0%]

Discovery question: What is your current forecast accuracy at the SKU/store level for lifestyle product lines — and what percentage of annual markdown cost traces directly back to forecast error?

Procurement & Trade Risk

A €200M tariff bill is baked into your 2026 guidance — and your sourcing team still responds in weeks.

Risk Mitigation Decision Cycle Compression

Primary buyers: CFO · COO · CSCO

  • › adidas embedded €200M in tariff headwinds into FY2026 guidance, triggering a €420M miss vs. analyst consensus and a 7% stock drop on results day despite record underlying performance. The gap is almost entirely tariff and FX — not operations. Management explicitly excluded potential upside if tariffs ease. [FACT — FY2025 results, March 4, 2026]
  • › Vietnam (27% of production) and Indonesia (19%) carry the same US tariff escalation risk that China carried in 2025 — combined, 46% of adidas's manufacturing base. There is no public evidence of a real-time scenario model that continuously calculates the landed cost impact of a Vietnam- or Indonesia-specific tariff shock. When the China shock hit in 2025, the sourcing pivot took months. Best-in-class teams with continuous scenario modeling respond in 48–72 hours. [FACT — sourcing shares, FY2025 Annual Report; INFERENCE — response gap]
  • › The Databricks multi-agent infrastructure that adidas is presenting at the 2026 Data+AI Summit is already capable of running a tariff scenario agent. The infrastructure cost is already paid. The decision model has not been built. [FACT — Databricks 2026 summit; INFERENCE — application gap]

Cost of Inaction

€200–300M / year

A Vietnam or Indonesia-specific tariff escalation equivalent to what China experienced could add €200–300M beyond the €200M already embedded in guidance. Every week without an automated response model is a week of unmanaged financial exposure at the CFO's desk. Section 122 tariff authority expires July 24, 2026 — a hard deadline with no confirmed defense. [FACT on confirmed €200M exposure; FACT on Section 122 expiry; INFERENCE on additional escalation risk]

Discovery question: How long does it take your procurement team today to go from a US tariff policy announcement to a final sourcing shift decision — and who owns that decision, at what cadence?

Consumer Fulfillment

Your 1,933 stores sit idle while customers wait 3–7 days. Amazon delivers in 1–2.

Revenue Protection / Growth Fulfillment Speed

Primary buyers: COO · CSCO · CMO · CEO

  • › adidas operates 1,933 own stores with RFID inventory tracking at greater than 99% accuracy — a confirmed, distributed fulfillment network sitting in the markets where adidas.com customers live. Ship-from-store technology is deployed. But the scale of active ship-from-store fulfillment has not been confirmed as operational at any meaningful volume. [FACT on infrastructure — FY2025 Annual Report; INFERENCE on operational scale]
  • › Standard US direct-to-consumer delivery is 3–7 days; 2-day nationwide via standard carrier options. Amazon Prime delivers in 1–2 days across 90%+ of US addresses. In Europe, Zalando delivers in 1–3 days with its own logistics network. adidas.com e-commerce is €4.3B and growing at +16% currency-neutral in FY2025. The delivery gap applies to the fastest-growing part of the business. [FACT — consumer fulfillment research; FACT on e-commerce metrics, FY2025 Annual Report]
  • › North America grew only +4% currency-neutral in FY2025 vs. +13% globally — and the FIFA World Cup in the US, Mexico, and Canada is 10–12 weeks away. The fulfillment gap is largest exactly where the commercial moment is largest. Every order that converts and arrives late is a loyalty data point that goes to Amazon instead of adidas. [FACT — FY2025 results; FACT on World Cup timing]

Cost of Inaction

€43M+ / year

adidas.com e-commerce at €4.3B and growing at 16%. Even a 1-percentage-point improvement in conversion from closing the delivery gap represents €43M in incremental revenue. The stores, the RFID, and the ship-from-store technology already exist. The routing intelligence does not. [FACT on e-commerce base — FY2025 Annual Report; INFERENCE on conversion leverage]

Discovery question: What percentage of adidas.com orders in the US are currently fulfilled from stores vs. central distribution centers — and what is the specific bottleneck preventing scale: routing software, store labor, carrier integration, or a commercial decision?

Inbound Logistics

You have real-time tracking across your supply chain and still pay €20–40M in avoidable air freight.

COGS Avoidance Response Latency

Primary buyers: CFO · CSCO · COO

  • › adidas adopted project44 for real-time supply chain visibility in February 2026 — under World Cup supply pressure. Real-time tracking and predictive decision-making are two different things. The visibility layer now exists across the inbound network. The decision model that uses it to avoid costly mode upgrades has not been confirmed as active. [FACT on project44 adoption — February 2026; INFERENCE on decision layer gap]
  • › Air freight runs at 4–6× the cost of ocean shipping. When a shipment misses its ocean window due to port congestion or factory delay, the reactive upgrade to air is typically a manual decision made days after the window has already closed. Industry benchmarks suggest 15–25% of air freight spend is avoidable with predictive mode optimization. With an estimated total air freight spend of €100–200M annually, a 20% reduction = €20–40M in direct COGS savings. [FACT — air/ocean rate premium; INFERENCE on avoidable share; ASSUMPTION on total air spend]
  • › 283 factories across Vietnam, Indonesia, China, and Cambodia ship into a 60-distribution-center global network. Port congestion at the top 5 Asia-to-North-America lanes is predictable 7–14 days in advance using the vessel queue data that project44 now provides. The data exists. The alert model does not. [FACT on factory and DC count — FY2025 Annual Report; INFERENCE on prediction capability]

Cost of Inaction

€20–40M / year

The infrastructure investment — project44 — is already paid. Every month without an activated decision model is a month of reactive air freight premiums that could have been avoided before the ship left the port. Additionally: each undetected inbound delay creates DC labor disruption, elevated safety stock requirements, and wholesale fill-rate penalties that do not appear in the freight line item. [INFERENCE — based on industry benchmark for logistics visibility programs; ASSUMPTION on spend base]

Discovery question: What is your current annual air freight spend as a share of total logistics cost — and how far in advance is the decision to upgrade a shipment from ocean to air currently made, and by whom?
